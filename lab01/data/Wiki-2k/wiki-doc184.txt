   Donald Louis Evans (born July 27, 1946) was the 34th [25]U.S. Secretary
   of Commerce. He was appointed by his longtime friend [26]George W. Bush
   and sworn into office on January 20, 2001. On November 9, 2004, the
   [27]White House announced that Evans intended to resign by the end of
   January 2005.

   Donald Evans was chosen to be the Non-Executive Chairman of [28]TXU
   Energy, following the completion of its acquisition by [29]Kohlberg
   Kravis Roberts and [30]Texas Pacific Group.

[[35]edit] Early life

   Born in [36]Houston, Texas, Evans attended the [37]University of Texas
   at Austin, receiving a [38]B.S. in [39]Mechanical Engineering in 1969
   and an [40]MBA in 1973. While at UT, he was a member of [41]Texas
   Cowboys, [42]Omicron Delta Kappa and the [43]Sigma Alpha Epsilon
   fraternity.

   In 1975, Evans moved to [44]Midland, Texas from Houston and began
   working on an [45]oil rig for Tom Brown Inc., a large independent
   energy company now based in [46]Denver. Ten years later he took the
   company over as CEO and continued running it until becoming Commerce
   Secretary.

[[47]edit] Political career

   In 1995, Bush, then the [48]Governor of Texas, appointed Evans to the
   Board of Regents of the [49]University of Texas System, serving as
   Chairman of the Board for the last four years. He was a board member of
   the [50]Scleroderma Research Foundation for eight years and a driving
   force behind Native Vision, a program that provides services to some
   10,000 [51]Native American children. He has been involved with the
   [52]United Way of America for many years, serving as President in 1989
   and Campaign Chair in 1981. He has been named [53]Jaycees Man of the
   Year.

   Evans worked for Bush's successful gubernatorial campaigns in 1994 and
   1998, and served as Chairman of the Bush/[54]Cheney 2000 campaign.
   During Bush's 2004 [55]State of the Union he was the [56]designated
   survivor and spent the night in a secure undisclosed location. In the
   event of an attack on the [57]Capitol, he would have become [58]Acting
   President.

   On November 9, 2004, Evans submitted his resignation from the post of
   Secretary of Commerce, although he stayed until his successor was sworn
   in on February 7, 2005.

   The [59]New York Times reported on May 26, 2006 that Evans had emerged
   as the front-runner to take over the [60]Treasury Department pending
   [61]John W. Snow's then rumored resignation. However, on May 30,
   [62]Henry Paulson was nominated to replace Snow.

   He currently heads up the committee to determine a location for the
   [63]presidential library to be built for George W. Bush upon the
   latter's leaving office.

   Evans also serves as CEO of the [64]Financial Services Forum, a trade
   association representing the CEOs of financial services firms dedicated
   to promoting policies that encourage an open and competitive global
   marketplace.

[[65]edit] Personal life

   He is married to Susan Marinis Evans. They have two daughters, a son
   and a granddaughter.
