   Tetragonia is a genus of 50-60 species of [18]flowering plants in the
   family [19]Aizoaceae, native to temperate and subtropical regions
   mostly of the [20]Southern Hemisphere, in [21]New Zealand,
   [22]Australia, southern [23]Africa and [24]South America. The best
   known species of Tetragonia is the leafy [25]vegetable [26]food
   [27]crop, [28]Tetragonia tetragonioides, or New Zealand spinach.

   Plants of the Tetragonia genus are [29]herbs or small [30]shrubs.
   [31]Leaves are alternate and [32]succulent, with flowers typically
   yellow and small . [33]Fruit are initially succulent but become dry and
   woody with age.^[34][1]

   New Zealand spinach is widely cultivated as a summer leafy vegetable.
