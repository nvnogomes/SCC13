   Released 1986
   Recorded [11]Thunderpants Studio, [12]San Pedro, California, [13]USA
   [14]Genre [15]Indie, [16]Experimental, [17]Punk
   Length 42:10
   [18]Label [19]New Alliance Records (NAR032)
   [20]Producer [21]Mike Watt, [22]Kira Roessler

   DOS was the first album recorded by the band [23]Dos. It is a curious
   yet interesting double bass guitar side project for [24]Mike Watt (of
   the [25]Minutemen and [26]fIREHOSE) and his former wife, [27]Kira
   Roessler (from [28]Black Flag). Formed as a vehicle to take the bass
   beyond its perceived background role and into the forefront of a band,
   Dos (named for the Spanish "two," not the operating system) also became
   a therapeutic experience in the wake of Roessler's departure from Black
   Flag and, more tragically, Minutemen [29]D. Boon's sudden death on
   December 22, 1985. After exchanging demos for the better part of 1986
   (Roessler had moved to Connecticut), the duo reconvened to record their
   self-titled debut late in the year, subsequently releasing it on Watt's
   own New Alliance Records.
