John Platt

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

     (Redirected from [6]Dr John Platt)
   Jump to: [7]navigation, [8]search

   John Platt is a senior [9]researcher in the [10]Knowledge Tools Group
   at [11]Microsoft Corporation. Platt has worked for Microsoft since
   1997. Prior to Microsoft, Platt had served as Director of Research at
   [12]Synaptics.

   Platt was born in [13]Elgin, Illinois and matriculated at
   [14]California State University, Long Beach at the age of 14. After
   graduating from CSULB at the age of 18, Platt enrolled in a computer
   science PhD program at [15]California Institute of Technology.

   While a student at Caltech under astronomer [16]Gene Shoemaker, Platt
   discovered two asteroids, one of which he named after his father (3237
   Victorplatt).

   In [17]August 2005, [18]Apple Computer had its application for a
   [19]patent on the interface of the popular [20]iPod music player
   rejected by the [21]United States Patent and Trademark Office. The
   reason appears to be that Platt had submitted a patent application for
   a similar interface design five months prior to Apple's claim. ^[22][1]

   Platt shared a 2005 Scientific and Technical Achievement Oscar from the
   [23]Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences with Demetri
   Terzopoulos for their pioneering work in physically-based
   computer-generated techniques used to simulate realistic cloth in
   motion pictures. ^[24][2]

   Platt has an [25]Erds-Bacon number of 6. ^[26][3]
