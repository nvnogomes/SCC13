   Mactridae, also known as trough shells or duck clams, is a [18]family
   of [19]marine [20]bivalve [21]clams of the [22]order [23]Veneroida.

   The shells are mostly large and ovate with a strong hinge, the teeth
   divided by a central spoon-shaped chondrophore. A long anterior and a
   similar posterior lateral in the left valve, fit between paired
   anterior and posterior laterals in the right valve, and, in addition,
   there is a small [24]bifid lamellate cardinal in each valve, anterior
   to the chondrophore.
