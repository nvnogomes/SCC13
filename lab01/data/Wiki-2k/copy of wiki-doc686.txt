   Eagle of Delight, painted by [13]Charles Bird King

   Eagle of Delight (Hayne Hudjihini) was one of the five wives of Chief
   [14]Shaumonekusse of the [15]Otoe tribe.

   In 1822, she accompanied her husband to [16]Washington D.C. where she
   met [17]James Monroe, the [18]President of the United States. She was
   described by those who met her as beautiful and charming. Her portrait
   was commissioned, and was painted by [19]Charles Bird King. She died of
   [20]measles shortly after her visit.

   Although her original portrait was destroyed in a fire at the
   [21]Smithsonian Institution in 1865, King's personal copy of it was
   given to the [22]White House in 1962. It now hangs in the White House
   library.
