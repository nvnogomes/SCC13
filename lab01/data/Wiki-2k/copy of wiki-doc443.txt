   "Du bist" ("You are") was the [16]Austrian entry at the [17]Eurovision
   Song Contest 2004, performed in [18]German by [19]Tie Break. It was the
   only song in the Austrian semi-finals of this year to be sung in
   [20]German.

   As [21]Alf Poier had pre-qualified Austria for the final, the song was
   performed second on the night (following [22]Spain's [23]Ramn with
   "[24]Para llenarme de ti" and preceding [25]Norway's [26]Knut Anders
   Srum with "[27]High"). At the close of voting, it had received 9
   points, placing 21st in a field of 24 and forcing Austria to qualify
   from the semi-final at their next appearance.

   The song proved slightly controversial before the Contest, with former
   entrants [28]Waterloo & Robinson (the band which finished second in
   Austria's qualification system) protesting as the song was longer than
   the legal 3 minutes (the CD version is clocked at between 3:09 and
   3:11, depending on the player). There were also claims of plagiarism.
   The protest was not upheld, and Tie Break represented Austria [29][1].
   The song itself is a [30]boy band ballad, with the singers describing
   the various qualities of their lovers.

   It was succeeded as [31]Austrian representative at the [32]2005 Contest
   by [33]Global Kryner with "[34]Y as".
