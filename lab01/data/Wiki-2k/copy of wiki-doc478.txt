   James Joseph "Duffy" Waldorf, Jr. (born [24]August 20, [25]1962) is an
   [26]American [27]professional golfer.

   Waldorf was born in [28]Los Angeles, [29]California. He attended
   [30]UCLA where he was on the golf team from 1982-1985. He was a
   two-time NCAA All-American, 1985 College Player of the Year, and was
   selected for the [31]Walker Cup.

   Waldorf turned professional in 1985 and joined the [32]PGA Tour.

   Waldorf is 67th on the all time career money winners list at
   $11,638,275, as of August 2008. He has featured in the top 50 of the
   [33]Official World Golf Rankings.

   Waldorf finished T-14 in the 2007 [34]Q-School to regain his 2008 PGA
   Tour card. However, after playing in only 4 events, he underwent knee
   surgery in May 2008.^[35][1]

   Waldorf is a connoisseur of fine wine and is well known for his
   trademark colorful caps and shirts. His wife and children write
   messages and reminders on the golf balls he uses each week.
