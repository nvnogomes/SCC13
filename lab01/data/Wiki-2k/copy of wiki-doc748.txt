   East Hempfield Township is a [26]township in west-central [27]Lancaster
   County, [28]Pennsylvania, [29]United States. At the 2000 census the
   population was 21,399.

   East Hempfield is one of the six immediate [30]suburbs of the city of
   [31]Lancaster, all sharing the same official designation as Lancaster,
   Pennsylvania by the [32]United States Postal Service.^[33][1]

[[41]edit] Geography

   According to the [42]U.S. Census Bureau, the township has a total area
   of 21.1 square miles (54.8 km), all of it land.

[[43]edit] Demographics

   As of the [44]census^[45][2] of 2000, there were 21,399 people, 8,552
   households, and 6,054 families residing in the township. The
   [46]population density was 1,011.6 people per square mile (390.6/km).
   There were 8,761 housing units at an average density of 414.2/sq mi
   (159.9/km). The racial makeup of the township was 93.45% [47]White,
   1.51% [48]Black or [49]African American, 0.11% [50]Native American,
   2.56% [51]Asian, 1.51% from [52]other races, and 0.86% from two or more
   races. 2.78% of the population were [53]Hispanic or [54]Latino of any
   race.

   There were 8,552 households out of which 30.9% had children under the
   age of 18 living with them, 62.0% were [55]married couples living
   together, 6.3% had a female householder with no husband present, and
   29.2% were non-families. 24.1% of all households were made up of
   individuals and 9.5% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age
   or older. The average household size was 2.47 and the average family
   size was 2.96.

   In the township the population was spread out with 23.9% under the age
   of 18, 6.7% from 18 to 24, 27.4% from 25 to 44, 26.7% from 45 to 64,
   and 15.4% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 41
   years. For every 100 females there were 96.2 males. For every 100
   females age 18 and over, there were 93.6 males.

   The median income for a household in the township was $54,431, and the
   median income for a family was $68,965. Males had a median income of
   $43,958 versus $29,496 for females. The [56]per capita income for the
   township was $28,192. About 1.8% of families and 3.9% of the population
   were below the [57]poverty line, including 4.1% of those under age 18
   and 5.1% of those age 65 or over.

[[58]edit] Notable residents

     * [59]Tom Herr, a former [60]Major League Baseball player and former
       manager of the local [61]Lancaster Barnstormers. Herr is currently
       employed with the [62]Washington Nationals single A affiliate
       [63]Hagerstown Suns.

     * [64]Aaron Herr, the oldest son of Tom Herr, currently signed with
       the [65]Louisville Bats of the [66]International League. Aaron
       played briefly with the Lancaster Barnstormers in 2005, and later
       joined the [67]Springfield Cardinals of the [68]Texas League.

[[69]edit] Points of interest

     * Amos Herr House & Park, an old farmhouse built in 1852 and donated
       by Amos Herr upon death. The house now serves as the East Hempfield
       Township municipal building.

     * Dutch Apple Dinner Theater, an amateur dinner theater.

     * [70]Park City Center, largest enclosed [71]shopping mall in
       [72]South Central Pennsylvania.
