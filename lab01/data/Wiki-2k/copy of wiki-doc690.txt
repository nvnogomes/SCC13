Ear drop

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

   Jump to: [6]navigation, [7]search
   It has been suggested that [8]Antipyrine and benzocaine ear drops be
   [9]merged into this article or section. ([10]Discuss)

   Ear drops are a form of medicine used to treat or prevent [11]ear
   infections, especially infections of the [12]outer ear and [13]ear
   canal ([14]Otitis externa).

   Bacterial infections are sometimes treated with antibiotics. Examples
   are:-
     * Gentisone HC ear drops (containing [15]Gentamicin and
       [16]hydrocortisone).
     * Ciproxin HC ear drops (containing [17]ciprofloxacin and
       [18]hydrocortisone).
     * Sofradex ear drops (containing [19]Framycetin Sulphate,
       [20]Gramicidin, [21]Dexamethasone/[22]sodium metasulphobenzoate,
       [23]Phenylethanol.^[24][1]
     * Kenacomb ear drops, containing [25]triamcinolone acetonide,
       [26]neomycin and [27]gramicidin ([28]antibiotics) and [29]nystatin
       ([30]antifungal).

   [31]Over the counter ear drops are also available, including spirit
   drops (alcohol solution), which dries out the ear, and drops such as
   [32]Aqua Ear (used for [33]swimmer's ear) containing a mixture of
   substances such as [34]alcohol and [35]acetic acid, to dry the ear and
   make it difficult for [36]microbes to grow.^[[37]citation needed]
