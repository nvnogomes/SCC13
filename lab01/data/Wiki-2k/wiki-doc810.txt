Economic and public affairs

   Economic and Public Affairs (EPA) is a subject taught in the junior
   forms of most [12]secondary schools in [13]Hong Kong, either as a
   separate subject or as a component of combined humanities or combined
   social studies. The subject is concerned with helping students to
   develop a basic understanding of economic and political issues, of the
   economic and political foundations of Hong Kong society, and of their
   responsibilities and rights as [14]citizens and [15]consumers. In many
   ways, it is similar to [16]Civics courses which are taught in many
   other countries, but the syllabus content is almost entirely related to
   local Hong Kong topics.

[[23]edit] Topics

   Topics covered in the EPA syllabus include the following:

[[24]edit] Form One

   [25]Rights and duties of Hong Kong residents.

   The status and immigration rights of Chinese and Non-Chinese, permanent
   and non-permanent residents.

   The [26]Demographics of Hong Kong.

   [27]Transportation in Hong Kong.

[[28]edit] Form Two

   The [29]Basic Law of Hong Kong and the concept of [30]One Country Two
   Systems. Hong Kong's [31]autonomy and the [32]Chinese Government's
   [33]sovereignty.

   The structure of the [34]Hong Kong Government.

   Law and Order in Hong Kong.

   [35]Hong Kong's Legal System and Judiciary

   [36]Education in Hong Kong.

[[37]edit] Form Three

   [38]Consumer education.

   The development and features of the [39]Economy of Hong Kong.

   Hong Kong as a [40]trade and [41]financecentre.

   Visible and invisible trade.

   Problems faced by industry and their effect upon [42]Employment in Hong
   Kong.

   The government's role in the economy.

[[43]edit] Options After Completing EPA

   After studying EPA in Forms One to Three, students who wish to continue
   exploring the themes covered by the subject can opt to study Economics
   or Principles of Accounts for their [44]HKCEE and [45]HKALE
   examinations, or may opt to take Liberal Studies for HKALE.
