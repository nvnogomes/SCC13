Patriarch Joseph II of Constantinople

   Joseph II of Constantinople, fresco by [9]Benozzo Gozzoli, [10]Cappella
   dei Magi, [11]Firenze

   Patriarch Joseph II (1360-1439) was [12]Patriarch of Constantinople
   from 1416-1439.

   Born the son of [13]Ivan Shishman of Bulgaria in 1360, little is known
   of his early life before he became a monk on [14]Mount Athos. He became
   Metropolitan of [15]Ephesus in 1393, before being elected Patriarch of
   Constantinople on 21 May 1416. Together with [16]Byzantine Emperor
   [17]John VIII Palaiologos, 23 Metropolitan bishops and countless
   scholars and theologians, he took part in the [18]Council of Florence.
   While in [19]Florence, he was quartered in the Palazzo Ferrantini. He
   is portrayed in [20]Benozzo Gozzoli's frescoes in the [21]Magi Chapel
   of [22]Palazzo Medici Riccardi, which celebrates the entrance of the
   Byzantine dignitaries in the city.

   Joseph was very old and ill and died within 2 months on 10 June 1439.
   His death caused much grief to all present at the Council, as he was a
   fervent supporter of union between the Churches. His grave in the
   [23]Dominican [24]convent church of [25]Santa Maria Novella in
   [26]Florence survives, with an elaborate [27]fresco portrait in a
   semi-Byzantine style. He was succeeded as Patriarch of Constantinople
   by [28]Metrophanes II, who was appointed by Emperor John VIII on
   account of his similarly pro-unionist sentiments.
