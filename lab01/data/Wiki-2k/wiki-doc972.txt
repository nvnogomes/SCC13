   Edwin Milton Abbott ([14]June 4, [15]1877 [16]November 8, [17]1940) was
   an [18]American lawyer and poet, born in [19]Philadelphia and educated
   at the Central High School there and the [20]University of
   Pennsylvania. He was admitted to the [21]bar in 1896 and subsequently
   distinguished himself in criminal cases. He was chief counsel in the
   fight of the Philadelphia commuters against the railroads, a member of
   the [22]Pennsylvania House of Representatives, 1911-12, chairman of the
   Commission on the Revision of Criminal Laws in the State of
   Pennsylvania, 1912-15 and 1917-23, and in 1913 minority nominee for
   judge of the Court of Common Pleas. He was appointed secretary of the
   American Institute of [23]Criminal Law and [24]Criminology in 1913.

   He was the author of Thoughts in Verse (1922) and The Law and Religion
   (1938).
