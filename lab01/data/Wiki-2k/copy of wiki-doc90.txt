   The Museum of London Docklands (formerly known as Museum in Docklands)
   is a museum on the [17]Isle of Dogs, [18]East London that tells the
   history of [19]London's [20]River Thames and [21]Docklands. The museum
   is part of the [22]Museum of London Group which is jointly funded by
   the [23]City of London Corporation and the [24]Greater London
   Authority.

   The museum opened in 2003 in a group of [25]grade I listed early 19th
   century [26]Georgian "low" sugar warehouses built in 1802^[27][1] on
   the side of [28]West India Docks on the Isle of Dogs, a short walk from
   the [29]Canary Wharf development.^[30][2]

   The nucleus of the collection is the museum and archives of the
   [31]Port of London Authority. These became part of the port and river
   collections of the [32]Museum of London in 1976, but largely remained
   in storage until the new museum was opened.

   The museum uses the latest presentational techniques including videos
   presented by [33]Tony Robinson, known for his involvement with [34]Time
   Team. There is a large collection of historical artefacts, models, and
   pictures. It is a substantial museum with 12 galleries and a children's
   gallery (Mudlarks),^[35][2] arranged over two of its floors. Visitors
   are directed through the displays in chronological order. The periods
   covered range from the first port of London in [36]Roman times to the
   closure of the central London docks in the 1970s and the subsequent
   transformation of the area with new commercial and residential
   developments.

   Museum of London Docklands includes a lecture theatre and meeting rooms
   and there are often talks and events connected with the docks. Several
   dock workers who worked on the docks in the sixties take part in these
   events, including one from the [37]Pentonville Five. There is also a
   reading room and the Sainsburys Study Centre where the public are
   welcome to consult the archives; a restaurant and shop.

   Admission is 5 for adults or 3 concs (over 60s, unwaged) and the ticket
   lasts for one year from the date of issue. Free entry for under 16s,
   NUS and disabled carers.
