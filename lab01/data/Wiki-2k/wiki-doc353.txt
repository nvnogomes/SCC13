   John Coleman (born 1935) is a former British Intelligence Officer for
   [13]MI6. He has written several books regarding conspiracies to create
   a [14]New World Order. These books have been influential among more
   well known conspiracy authors such as [15]David Icke and [16]Jim Marrs.
   Coleman argues that the [17]Muslim Brotherhood is a secret [18]Masonic
   order created, with support from [19]T. E. Lawrence, [20]Bertrand
   Russell and [21]St. John Philby, to "keep the [22]Middle East backward
   so its natural resource, oil, could continue to be looted."^[23][1]
   Coleman has also criticized the [24]Club of Rome, the [25]Giorgio Cini
   Foundation, [26]Global 2000, the [27]Interreligious Peace Colloquium,
   the [28]Tavistock Institute, and other organizations.

[[29]edit] Book

   Coleman has published 12 books and hundreds of [30]white paper reports
   -- and currently publishes the World In Review magazine. The titles
   include:
     * The Tavistock Institute Of Human Relations: Shaping the Moral,
       Spiritual, Cultural, Political and Economic Decline of the United
       States of America
     * Conspirators Hierarchy: The Story of the [31]Committee of 300
     * One World Order: Socialist Dictatorship (formerly titled Socialism:
       The Road To Slavery)
     * Diplomacy by Deception: An Account of the Treasonous Conduct by the
       Governments of Britain and the United States
     * Beyond The Conspiracy: Unmasking The Invisible World Government,
       The Committee Of 300
     * What You Should Know About The United States Constitution And The
       Bill Of Rights
