   Dueling Analogs (a pun on the [18]Dual Analog Controller) is a
   [19]webcomic by Steve Napierski (also the creator of [20]The Outer
   Circle). The website was launched on November 17, 2005, and new comics
   are posted every Monday and Thursday. Dueling Analogs is a gaming comic
   and as such most of the comics take place in the universe of the games
   discussed. There are few ongoing stories or plotlines as all of the
   comics are self-encapsulated (one exception is the trial of King Bowser
   ^[21][1]); however, there are repeated themes such as 'Rejected Mega
   Man Villains' ^[22][2], 'Games that I am glad were never made' ^[23][3]
   and 'What if...?' ^[24][4]. Dueling Analogs is also a founding member
   of a webcomic group of gaming comics. ^[25][5] The comic came to
   prominence in the webcomic community after the "So Dark the Contra of
   Man" strip.^[26][6]

[[36]edit] Tagline

   Dueling Analogs is a color semi-weekly webcomic that lampoons the
   characters, culture and subtext of modern gaming culture

[[37]edit] Characters

     * Harvey - Harvey is the voice of Dueling Analogs. Whenever the comic
       needs to talk directly to the audience about a topic, Harvey is
       there. Harvey's design is modeled after the look of its creator
       from his high school years. This is in contrast to the character
       Steve from The Outer Circle which was modeled after a more recent
       look of the creator.

                First Appearance: [38]2005-[39]11-29, [40]strip 4.

     * Mario - Though not created by Steve Napierski, [41]Mario has
       appeared in so many Dueling Analogs strips that he is considered a
       regular member of the comics cast.

                First Appearance: [42]2005-[43]12-01, [44]strip 5.

     * Jeremy the Sony PR Gnome - The voice of [45]Sony. Jeremy acts as
       the counterpoint to the voice of Harveys pro-[46]Nintendo views.
       Could possibly be a parody of the gnome from [47]Amlie or the gnome
       feature in the [48]Travelocity commercials: [49]Where is my Gnome?.

                First Appearance: [50]2006-[51]07-31, [52]strip 76.

     * Xed Box - If [53]Microsoft had a spokesperson in Dueling Analogs,
       Xed Box would be it. Unfortunately, Xed Box is nothing more than a
       box with a crudely drawn "X" on it.

                First Appearance: [54]2006-[55]08-16, [56]strip 81.

     * Jon Shelf American - Jon Shelf American is what's considered
       Dueling Analogs` stereotypical gamer (introvert, unsociable and
       still living at home with his mommy). Name based on [57]George
       Liquor American created by [58]John Kricfalusi.

                First Appearance: [59]2007-[60]01-15, [61]strip 130.

[[67]edit] Parodies

   Dueling Analogs has featured such games as [68]Final Fantasy VII
   ^[69][7], [70]The Smurfs ^[71][8], [72]Mega Man ^[73][2], [74]Karnov
   ^[75][9], [76]Super Smash Brothers ^[77][10] and [78]Final Fantasy X-2
   ^[79][11].

[[80]edit] Awards and Nominations

   Awards

       Design ^[84][13]

   readers of [85]Joystiq on nine occasions: May 23, 2006 ^[86][14], June
   3, 2006 ^[87][15], June 24, 2006 ^[88][16], February 13, 2007
   ^[89][17], March 27, 2007 ^[90][18], May 22, 2007 ^[91][19], October
