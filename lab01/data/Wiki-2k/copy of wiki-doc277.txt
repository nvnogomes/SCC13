Vaginal septum

   A vaginal septum is a congenital partition within the [16]vagina; such
   a septum could be either longitudinal or transverse.

[[21]edit] Longitudinal septum - double vagina

   A longitudinal vaginal septum develops during [22]embryogenesis when
   there is an incomplete fusion of the lower parts of the two
   [23]mullerian ducts. As a result there is a double vagina.^[24][1]
   There may be associated duplications of the more cranial parts of the
   mullerian derivatives, a double [25]cervix, and either a [26]uterine
   septum or [27]uterus didelphys (double uterus).^[28][2]^[29][3]

   The person with a longitudinal vaginal septum may be asymptomatic and
   not aware of the condition. If [30]dyspareunia is a problem a simple
   resection of the septum could be performed.

[[31]edit] Transverse septum

   A transverse septum can form during embryogenesis when the mullerian
   ducts fuse improperly to the [32]urogenital sinus. A complete
   transverse will block menstrual flow and is a cause of primary
   [33]amenorrhea. The accumulation of menstrual debris behind the septum
   is termed [34]cryptomenorrhea. Some transverse septa are incomplete and
   may lead to dysparunia or obstruction in [35]labor.^[36][4] A surgical
   incision will relieve the situation.
