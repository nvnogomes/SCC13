Disputation of the Holy Sacrament

   Disputation of the Sacrament
   [8]Raphael, [9]1509-[10]1510
   [11]Fresco
   500 770 cm
   [12]Apostolic Palace, [13]Vatican City

   The Disputation of the Sacrament ([14]Italian: La disputa del
   sacramento), or Disputa, is a [15]painting by the [16]Italian
   [17]Renaissance artist [18]Raphael. It was painted between [19]1509 and
   [20]1510 as only the first part of Raphael's commission to decorate
   with [21]frescoes the rooms that are now known as the [22]Stanze di
   Raffaello, in the [23]Apostolic Palace in the [24]Vatican. At the time,
   this room was know as the [25]Stanza della segnatura, and was the
   private papal library where the supreme papal tribunal met (Adams 344).

   This summarizes the work, drawing from the work of art historian Laurie
   Adams. In the painting, Raphael has created a scene spanning both
   heaven and earth. Above, [26]Christ is surrounded by the [27]Blessed
   Virgin Mary, [28]John the Baptist and various biblical figures such as
   [29]Adam, [30]Moses and [31]Jacob. God sits above Jesus, depicted
   reigning over the golden light of heaven. Below, on the altar sits the
   [32]monstrance. The [33]altar is flanked by theologians who are
   depicted debating [34]Transubstantiation (Adams 345). Christ's body is
   represented in the [35]eucharist, which is discussed by representatives
   of the Church; among them are [36]Pope Julius II, [37]Pope Sixtus IV,
   [38]Savonarola and [39]Dante Alighieri. Pope Sixtus IV is the gold
   dressed pope in the bottom of the painting. Directly behind Sixtus is
   Dante, wearing red and sporting a laurel wreath (symbolizing his
   greatness as a writer) (Adams 346). In the left hand corner, there is a
   bald figure reading a book leaning over a railing. This is Raphael's
   mentor and famous renaissance architect [40]Bramante.
