MV Doulos

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

   MV Doulos (at [9]Southampton, [10]England in 2004)

   The MV Doulos is the world's oldest active [11]ocean-faring
   [12]passenger ship. She is now owned by the [13]German [14]charity
   [15]Gute Bcher fr Alle (Good Books for All), and is used as a floating
   [16]bookshop. The ship has previously been been known as the SS Medina,
   the SS Roma, and the MV Franca C.

[[22]edit] History

   The Medina was built in 1914 by [23]Newport News Shipbuilding and Dry
   Dock Company for the [24]Mallory Steamship Company of the [25]United
   States. She was a [26]freighter serving the [27]Atlantic; during
   [28]World War II she served with the [29]United States Coast Guard.

   The [30]Panamanian company Naviera San Miguel SA acquired the Medina in
   1948; they renamed the ship the Roma, and converted her into a
   passenger ship with cabins for 287 people, and dormitories for an
   additional 694 people.

   In 1952 Naviera San Miguel resold the Roma to [31]Linea Costa, an
   [32]Italian company. At this time the SS Roma, a [33]steamship, was
   converted into a motor vessel and renamed the MV Franca C. She carried
   passengers between [34]Italy and [35]Argentina. In 1959, the Franca C
   was adapted into a [36]cruise liner, principally cruising the
   [37]Mediterranean.

   In 1977, Gute Bcher fr Alle acquired the Franca C, and renamed her the
   Doulos (Greek for servant). In her current role, she is manned by a
   [38]volunteer crew and visits [39]sea ports world-wide.

   The MV Doulos currently holds the biggest floating library in the
   world. Normally there is somewhere around 3000 to 5000 books on the
   shelf and half a million in the hold.^[[40]citation needed]

   Doulos is currently in Bacolod City, Philippines until March 2. For
   details on their current port and a list of future ports, visit their
   [41]official website.

   She is currently doing her last world tour and is planned to be
   de-commissioned in 2010.
