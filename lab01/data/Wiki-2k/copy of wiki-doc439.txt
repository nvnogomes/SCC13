Drying cabinet

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

   Jump to: [6]navigation, [7]search
   A drying cabinet

   A drying cabinet is an electronic [8]machine designed to expedite the
   drying of items - usually [9]clothing - that are unsuitable for a
   traditional [10]clothes dryer. Such items may include delicate clothing
   designated as "hang dry, "dry flat", or "do not tumble dry" on their
   wash instructions, as well as items such as comforters, boots, and
   coats.

[[15]edit] History

   Since the 1980s, drying cabinets have been common in [16]European
   [17]laundromats, especially in the [18]Swedish [19]Tvttstuga - a
   [20]laundry room in Swedish [21]housing cooperatives. With the large
   size of the drying cabinets and relatively small size of European
   homes, drying cabinets have been almost exclusively found in
   self-service laundry facilities.

[[22]edit] History in the U.S.

   With the steadily increasing size of [23]American homes over the past
   decades, the laundry room has increased in size and functionality as
   well. To meet the increasing [24]demand for luxury appliances in the US
   residential market, a handful of [25]appliance [26]manufacturers
   (including [27]Asko and [28]Staber) have begun to supply drying
   cabinets for the home within the last few years. A typical residential
   drying cabinet is approximately the size of a narrow [29]refrigerator,
   and is used to supplement traditional tumble dryers.
