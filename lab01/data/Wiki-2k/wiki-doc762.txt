   East Tytherley is a small village in [13]Hampshire, [14]England.

   The village was given to [15]Queen Philippa by her husband [16]Edward
   III in 1335. When the [17]Black Death spread through [18]London she
   moved her [19]court to the village.

   In more recent history [20]William Fothergill Cooke invented the first
   commercial [21]electrical telegraph whilst living in the village.
