   The Netherlands East Indies campaign of 194142 was the short-lived
   defence of the [49]Netherlands East Indies (Indonesia) by [50]Allied
   forces, against invasion by the [51]Empire of Japan in [52]World War
   II. The rich [53]oil resources of Indonesia were a major Japanese
   objective during the war. The campaign and subsequent three and a half
   year [54]Japanese occupation contributed to the end of [55]Dutch
   colonial rule in Indonesia.

   General [56]Hisaichi Terauchi (also known as Count Terauchi), who was
   the commander of the [57]Southern Expeditionary Army Group, began the
   campaign with attacks against [58]Borneo: on December 16, 1941,
   Japanese forces successfully occupied [59]Miri, an oil production
   centre in northern [60]Sarawak.

   [61]Dutch, Australian, British and United States forces fought the
   Japanese during the campaign. From January 1, 1942, Allied forces in
   [62]South East Asia formed the [63]American-British-Dutch-Australian
   Command (ABDACOM), under the British General [64]Archibald Wavell.
   ABDACOM's control of the "Malay Barrier" (also known as the "East
   Indies Barrier") was considered vital to the Allies' global strategy.
   However, Japanese advances over the next several weeks split the Allied
   forces, and ABDACOM was dissolved on February 25. Allied operations in
   Indonesia (except [65]Sumatra) were later controlled by the [66]South
   West Pacific Area command, under General [67]Douglas MacArthur.

   The campaign includes
