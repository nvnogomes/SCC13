   East Africa Time, or EAT, is a time zone used in eastern [28]Africa.
   The zone is three hours ahead of UTC ([29]UTC+3), which is the same as
   [30]Moscow Time, and also the same as [31]Eastern European Summer Time.

   As this time zone is predominantly in the [32]equatorial region, there
   is no significant change in day length throughout the year, so
   [33]daylight saving time is not observed.

   East Africa Time is used by the following countries:
     *  [34]Comoros
     *  [35]Djibouti
     *  [36]Eritrea
     *  [37]Ethiopia
     *  [38]Kenya
     *  [39]Madagascar
     *  [40]Somalia
     *  [41]Sudan
     *  [42]Tanzania
     *  [43]Uganda
