   Escallonia is a genus of [20]flowering plants of the [21]Escalloniaceae
   family. Commonly used as a hedging plant, it grows about 1 ft per year,
   and reaches between 4-8ft in height. It is unhappy in coastal areas,
   but not very tolerant of dry winds.

   Flowering: June-October It has masses of rosy crimson flowers, with a
   honey fragrance, and grows between 5-10ft.

   The plant 'escallonia' is mentioned in [22]Virginia Wolfe's book
   'Jacob's Room'. The plant escallonia is incapable of living next to any
   body of water, particularly salt water. It therefore symbolizes 'order'
   in her book.
   A flowering Escallonia cultivar dominates the center of this cul-de-sac
   in [23]Sidney, British Columbia.
   A closer view of the cul-de-sac Escallonia shown above.
