Pierre-Antoine Gourgaud

   Pierre-Antoine Gourgaud ([14]29 April [15]1706, [16]Paris - [17]1 March
   [18]1774, Paris), stage name Dugazon pre (to distinguish him from his
   son [19]Dugazon), was a French actor.

   Patriarch of a large acting [20]dynasty, Dugazon advised [21]Louis XIV
   on buildings, bridges and lodges. He left his birthplace around 1730
   and it was probably then that he became an actor. He married
   Marie-Catherine Dumay in [22]Lille on [23]18 November [24]1734 and
   stayed in that city until 1739, apart from a two year stay in
   [25]Brussels as director of the [26]Thtre de la Monnaie. On [27]11
   December [28]1739, he dbuted at the [29]Comdie-Franaise but was not
   received into its company.

   From 1742 to 1749, he acted in [30]Marseille where, among other
   theatrical engagements, he was director of hospitals for the [31]arme
   d'Italie. Returning to his nomadic lifestyle, he acted at [32]Bordeaux
   and [33]Bayonne, before being engaged in [34]Stuttgart in 1760. He then
   directed the theatre at [35]Montpellier during the 1769-1770 season and
   died in Paris in 1774.

   At least two of his children took up a theatrical career :
     * [36]Franoise-Rose Gourgaud (1743-1804)
     * [37]Jean-Henri Gourgaud (1746-1809)
