Edward Bierstadt

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

   Jump to: [6]navigation, [7]search

   Edward Bierstadt (1824 1906) was an [8]American [9]portrait and
   [10]landscape [11]photographer, and [12]engraver. He was a brother of
   the painter [13]Albert Bierstadt, and made a number of engravings of
   his brother's work. He opened his own studio in [14]New York City in
   1860.

   He was hired by [15]William West Durant to take a series of photos for
   an advertising brochure entitled The Adirondacks, Artotype Views Among
   the Mountains and Lakes of the North Woods to publicize [16]Blue
   Mountain Lake and [17]Raquette Lake in the [18]Adirondacks.

   Bierstadt held an 1876 patent for an improvement to the
   [19]Stereoscope.
