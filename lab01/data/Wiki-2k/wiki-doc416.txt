Drosera spatulata

   Drosera spatulata
   [8]Scientific classification
   Kingdom: [9]Plantae
   Order:   [10]Caryophyllales
   Family:  [11]Droseraceae
   Genus:   [12]Drosera
   Species: D. spatulata
   [13]Binomial name
   Drosera spatulata
   [14]Labill. (1804)

   Drosera spatulata the spoon-leaved sundew is a variable,
   rosette-forming [15]sundew with spoon-shaped leaves -the [16]specific
   epithet is [17]Latin for "spatula shaped" - a reference to the form of
   the leaves^[18][1]. This sundew has a large range and occurs naturally
   throughout south-east [19]Asia, in southern [20]China and [21]Japan,
   [22]Micronesia, [23]New Guinea through to the eastern territories of
   [24]Australia and [25]Tasmania and [26]New Zealand.^[27][1] Variants
   are often known by the localities in which they are found. The plant
   does not form [28]hibernacula in winter, and is easily grown using the
   same methods as [29]Drosera capensis.

   Carnivorous plant growers consider Drosera spatulata to be a weed
   because it is very hardy and produces large amounts of seed when it
   flowers. The seeds also [30]germinate without much difficulty, causing
   little plants to pop up in surrounding pots.
