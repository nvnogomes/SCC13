Edward Breitung

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

   Jump to: [6]navigation, [7]search

   Edward Breitung ([8]November 10, [9]1831[10]March 3, [11]1887) was a
   [12]politician from the [13]U.S. state of [14]Michigan.

   Breitung, the son of John M. Breitung, a [15]Lutheran minister, was
   born in the city of [16]Schalkau in the Duchy of [17]Saxe-Meiningen,
   [18]Germany (now in the [19]Sonneberg of [20]Thuringia). He attended
   the College of Mining in [21]Meiningen, then one of the celebrated
   schools in Germany for scientific and classical studies. In 1849, after
   the [22]revolution in Germany, he immigrated to the [23]United States
   and settled in [24]Kalamazoo County, Michigan. He moved to [25]Detroit
   in 1851 and became a clerk in a mercantile house. He moved to
   [26]Marquette and engaged in mercantile pursuits until 1859, when he
   went to [27]Negaunee. He sold out his mercantile business to engage
   exclusively in iron-mining operations in 1864, explored for iron
   deposits in [28]Marquette and [29]Menominee Counties, locating several
   profitable mines from 1864 to 1867. He later became interested in gold
   and silver mining in [30]Colorado.

   Breitung was a member of the [31]Michigan State House of
   Representatives in 1873 and 1874 and a member of the [32]Michigan State
   Senate in 1877 and 1878. He served as mayor of Negaunee in 1879, 1880,
   and 1882. He was elected as a [33]Republican to the [34]United States
   House of Representatives from [35]Michigan's 11th congressional
   district for the [36]Forty-eighth Congress, serving from [37]March 4,
   [38]1883 to [39]March 3, [40]1885. He declined to be a candidate for
   renomination in 1884.

   Breitung died in Negaunee and is interred in Park Cemetery in
   Marquette. Breitung's son, Edward N. Breitung, continued his father's
   successful mining enterprises in the [41]Upper Peninsula of Michigan.

   [42]Breitung Township, Minnesota is named after him for his work in
   developing the Soudan Mine there in the 1880s. [43]Breitung Township,
   Michigan is also named for him.
