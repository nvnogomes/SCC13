   Doxylamine succinate is one of the many sedating [37]antihistamines
   used by itself as a short-term [38]sedative, in combination with other
   drugs as a night-time [39]cold and [40]allergy relief drug, and a
   preparation is prescribed in combination with [41]vitamin B6
   ([42]pyridoxine) to prevent [43]morning sickness in [44]pregnant women.

[[52]edit] Indications

   Doxylamine is a member of the [53]ethanolamine class of antihistamines
   and has anti-allergy power superior to almost every other antihistamine
   on the market, with the exception of [54]diphenhydramine
   ([55]Benadryl).^[56][2] It is also the most effective over-the-counter
   sedative available in the [57]United States,^[58][2] and is more
   sedating than some prescription [59]hypnotics. In a study, it was found
   that doxylamine [60]succinate is possibly more effective than the
   [61]barbiturate [62]phenobarbital for use as a sedative.^[63][2] For
   this reason, doxylamine has sometimes been used off label in a manner
   similar to diphenhydramine for the reduction of [64]anxiety symptoms.

   The dosage required to induce hypnosis ([65]sleep) can be as low as
   6.25 mg, but is usually effective in dosages of up to 25 mg. Higher
   doses are not recommended by the United States [66]Food and Drug
   Administration, although single dosage recommendations of up to 50 mg
   are common in some countries, including Australia, where it is marketed
   under the names Restavit and Dozile.

[[67]edit] Metabolites

   The 2 main metabolites are desmethyldoxylamine and
   didesmethyldoxylamine.

[[68]edit] Formulations

     * It is the sedating ingredient of [69]NyQuil.
     * In [70]Commonwealth countries, such as [71]Australia, [72]Canada,
       and the [73]United Kingdom, doxylamine is available prepared with
       [74]paracetamol (acetaminophen) and [75]codeine under the brand
       name Dolased , Syndol , or Mersyndol, as treatment for [76]tension
       headache and other types of [77]pain.
     * Doxylamine succinate is used in general [78]over-the-counter
       sleep-aids branded as Somnil, Dozile, Donormyl, Dormidina, Restavit
       and Unisom-2.
     * In the [79]United States doxylamine succinate is the active
       ingredient in the over-the-counter sleep-aid tablets branded as
       [80]Unisom; however, according to the [81]manufacturer's website,
       the [82]gel-cap form contains [83]diphenhydramine hydrochloride
       instead.
     * It is also available in combination with vitamin B[6] and [84]folic
       acid under the brand name Evanorm (marketed by Ion Healthcare).
     * In [85]Canada, doxylamine succinate and [86]pyridoxine ([87]vitamin
       B6) are the ingredients of Diclectin, which is used to prevent
       [88]morning sickness.

[[89]edit] Side effects

   Doxylamine succinate is a potent [90]anticholinergic and has a
   [91]side-effect profile common to such drugs, including [92]dry mouth,
   [93]ataxia, [94]urinary retention, and drowsiness. Other drugs with
   well known anticholinergic effects include many [95]antipsychotic and
   [96]tricyclic antidepressant drugs.

[[97]edit] Interactions

   [98]Venlafaxine combined with Doxylamine succinate can lead to
   dizziness, light-headedness, confusion and lethargy, along with the
   side-effects listed above.

[[99]edit] Toxicity

   Doxylamine succinate is generally safe for administration to healthy
   adults. Typical preparations that contain doxylamine range from 6.25 mg
   to 25.0 mg. The [100]LD50 is estimated to be 50500 mg/kg in
   humans.^[101][3] Symptoms of overdose may include dry mouth, dilated
   pupils, insomnia, euphoria, hallucinations, and seizures. Fatalities,
   while rare, have been recorded.^[102][4]
