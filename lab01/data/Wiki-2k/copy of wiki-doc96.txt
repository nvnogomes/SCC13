Doctor of Civil Law

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

   Jump to: [6]navigation, [7]search

   Some universities, such as the [8]University of Oxford, award Doctor of
   Civil Law (DCL) degrees instead of [9]Doctor of Laws (LL.D.) degrees.

   At Oxford, the degree of Doctor of Civil Law by Diploma is customarily
   conferred on foreign Heads of State, as well as on the Chancellor of
   the University. (The British Sovereign is unable to receive university
   degrees, since these would, theoretically, place her under the
   jurisdiction of the Chancellor of the university. Prior to her
   accession, the present Queen did accept several honorary degrees,
   including an Oxford DCL in 1948).^[10][1]

   The degree is a higher doctorate usually awarded on the basis of
   exceptionally insightful and distinctive publications that contain
   significant and original contributions to the study of law or politics
   in general. The degree of [11]Doctor of Canon Law was replaced by the
   DCL after [12]the Reformation.

   The DCL is senior to all degrees save the [13]Doctor of Divinity which
   was traditionally the highest degree bestowed by the Universities.

   In some other universities the DCL is an [14]honorary degree.

   A list of other higher institutions that grant DCL:
     * [15]University of Kent - [16]Kent, [17]United Kingdom
     * [18]McGill University Law School - [19]Montreal, Quebec [20]Canada
     * Louisiana State University [21]Paul M. Hebert Law Center -
       [22]Baton Rouge, [23]Louisiana
