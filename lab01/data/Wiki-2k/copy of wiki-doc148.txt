Dominique Auguste Lereboullet

   Dominique Auguste Lereboullet was a [13]French doctor and
   [14]zoologist. He was born on [15]19 September 1804 at [16]pinal, and
   died of an [17]apoplectic fit on [18]6 October 1865 in [19]Strasbourg.

   He began his studies at [20]Colmar before specialising in [21]medicine
   at Strasbourg where he graduated as a doctor with a thesis about
   [22]cholera. While practising medicine, he kept up a series of studies
   into [23]comparative anatomy of animals. On the departure of
   [24]Georges Louis Duvernoy to [25]Paris, Lereboullet took the
   opportunity to occupy the chair of zoology and comparative anatomy at
   the faculty of science in Strasbourg.

   His zoological studies included works on the genitals of vertebrates,
   [26]foie gras, comparative [27]embryology of [28]fish and
   [29]carcinology.
