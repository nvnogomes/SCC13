EBS International Documentary Festival

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

   Jump to: [6]navigation, [7]search

   The EBS International Documentary Festival (EIDF) is a film festival
   hosted by EBS Korea, a national public television featuring educational
   and cultural programs. EIDF has taken place annually in Seoul, South
   Korea, since 2004. EIDF was initiated to seek alternative answers to
   the widespread commercialism which dominates the current broadcasting
   environment, through documentary films. The festival not only screens a
   wide range of carefully selected documentaries, but also proffers
   educational forums and seminars, photo exhibitions, and master classes
   with prominent directors. EIDF 2008 is to be held from September 22nd
   to 28th under the theme of "Colors 360."

[[16]edit] 2004

     * Theme: Challenging Asia
     * Jury:

          + Trinh T. Minh-ha
          + Sandra Ruch
          + Wang Xiao Long
          + Dan Wolman
          + Do Jung Il

     * Awards:

          + Grand Prix: Last House Standing (Gan Chao, China, 2004)
          + Spirit Award: Journeys (Vinayan Kodoth, India, 2003)
          + Special Jury Award: Experimental Taiwanese Moon Academy (Wu
            Mi-Sen, Taiwan, 2003)
          + Audience Award: Mr. Cortisone, Happy Days (Shlomi Shir and
            Duki Dror, Israel, 2004)

     * Theme: Peace & Life in Asia
     * Jury:

          + Chong Heup Cho

     * Awards:

            2004)
          + Spirit Award: Little Birds (Takeharu Watai, Japan, 2005)
          + Special Jury Award: The Concrete Revolution (Xialou Guo,
            China, 2004)
          + Audience Award: School Among Glaciers (Dorji Wangchuk, Bhutan,
            2005)

[[18]edit] 2006

     * July 10-July 16
     * Theme: Prospering Asia, Reconciliation & Coexistence
     * Jury:

          + Dimitri Eipides
          + Sato Makoto
          + Claire Aguilar
          + Yoav Shamir
          + Lee Sung-moo

     * Awards:

          + Grand Prix: Out of Bounds (Alexandre Leborgne and Pierre
            Barougier, France, 2005)
          + Spirit Award: Innocence (Nisa Kongsri and Areeya Chumsai,
            Thailand, 2005)
          + Special Jury Award: Smiling in a War Zone (Simone Aaberg Kaern
            and Magnus Bejmar, Denmark, 2005)
          + Audience Award: Our Own Private Bin Laden (Samira Goetschel,
            Canada, Switzerland, 2005)
