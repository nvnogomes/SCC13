Doron Zeilberger

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

   Jump to: [6]navigation, [7]search
   Photograph of Doron Zeilberger wearing a T-shirt with a
   [8]Hypergeometric identity at its forefront.

   Doron Zeilberger ( , born [9]July 2, [10]1950 in [11]Israel) is an
   [12]Israeli mathematician, known for his work in [13]combinatorics.

   He is a Board of Governors Professor of Mathematics at [14]Rutgers
   University.^[15][1] He received his doctorate from the [16]Weizmann
   Institute of Science in [17]1976, under the direction of [18]Harry Dym.

   Zeilberger has made numerous important contributions to combinatorics,
   [19]hypergeometric identities, and [20]q-series. Zeilberger gave the
   first proof of the [21]alternating sign matrix conjecture, noteworthy
   not only for its mathematical content, but also for the fact that
   Zeilberger recruited nearly a hundred volunteer checkers to
   "pre-referee" the paper. Together with [22]Herbert Wilf, Zeilberger was
   awarded the [23]American Mathematical Society's [24]Leroy P. Steele
   Prize for Seminal Contributions to Research in [25]1998 for their
   development of [26]WZ theory, which has revolutionized the field of
   hypergeometric summation. In 2004, Zeilberger was awarded the [27]Euler
   Medal; the [28]citation refers to him as "a champion of using computers
   and algorithms to do mathematics quickly and efficiently."

   Zeilberger considers himself a [29]ultrafinitist^[30][2]. He is also
   known for crediting his computer "[31]Shalosh B. Ekhad" as a co-author
   ("Shalosh" and "Ekhad" mean "Three" and "One" in Hebrew respectively,
   referring to the [32]AT&T [33]3B1 model), and for his provocative
   [34]opinions, some of which are:
     * [35]"People who believe that applied math is bad math are bad
       mathematicians"
     * [36]"Guess what? Programming is even more fun than proving, and,
       more importantly it gives as much, if not more, insight and
       understanding"
     * [37]"Frank Quinn's rigor is not as rigorous as he thinks"
     * [38]"Still like that old-time blackboard talk"
