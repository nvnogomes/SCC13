   EMAP is a [22]British media company, specialising in the production of
   [23]business-to-business magazines, and the organisation of business
   events and conferences. The Company was once a constituent of the
   [24]FTSE 100 Index but is now owned by [25]Apax and [26]Guardian Media
   Group.

[[35]edit] History

   "EMAP" is an abbreviation of East Midland Allied Press.

   [36]Richard Winfrey purchased the [37]Spalding Guardian in 1887 and
   later purchased the [38]Lynn News and the [39]Peterborough Advertiser;
   he also started the [40]North Cambs Echo. Sir Richard Winfrey (1858-
   1944) was a [41]Liberal politician and campaigner for agricultural
   rights and the papers were used to promote his political views in and
   around [42]Spalding, [43]Boston, [44]Sleaford & [45]Peterborough.
   During World War II Winfrey's newspaper interests began to be passed
   over to his son, [46]Richard Pattinson Winfrey (1902-1985). In 1947,
   under the direction of 'Pat' Winfrey, the family's newspaper titles
   were consolidated to form the East Midland Allied Press. The company
   was formed by the merger of the Northamptonshire Printing and
   Publishing Co., the Peterborough Advertiser Co., the West Norfolk and
   King's Lynn Newspaper Co. and commercial printing sections at
   [47]Rushden, [48]King's Lynn and [49]Bury St. Edmunds.

   The magazine division was founded on a hunch when the company's
   printing presses lay dormant between printing issues of the local
   papers. The staff gambled that a weekly angling publication would be a
   hit - and in 1953 [50]Angling Times was born.^[51][1] This was soon
   joined by another weekly heavyweight when EMAP bought [52]Motor Cycle
   News from its founder in 1956 for a hundred pounds.^[53][1] It had been
   launched two years earlier. Both remain in the top 10 profit earners
   for the company (now Bauer) to this day. The Winfrey family continued
   to work on the management team of EMAP until the early 1980s and
   remained large shareholders until two thirds of the company were sold
   to Bauer Media Group in early 2008

   EMAP eventually beacaume a PLC in the late 1970's under the guidance of
   the extremely sucessful partnership of [54]Sir Robin Miller & [55]David
   Arculus.

   In 1996 EMAP PLC agreed to sell its 65 newspaper titles, including the
   300-year-old [56]Stamford Mercury, to [57]Johnston Press for 111
   million.^[58][2]

   On 27 July 2007, Emap announced that it was undertaking a review of the
   structure of the group in response to receiving a number of unsolicited
   proposals to purchase parts of the company.^[59][3]

   On 12 September 2007, Emap announced that it had completed the disposal
   of its Australian consumer magazine division, Emap Australia for
   approximately 38m to [60]ACP Magazines Ltd.^[61][4]

   On 29 January 2008, Emap PLC completed the sale of its
   [62]radio,[63]television and consumer media businesses to German
   company [64]Bauer for 1.14bn.^[65][5]

   This took the family-owned Bauer group to the largest media company in
   Europe.

   The remainder of the company was taken over by Eden Bidco Ltd, a
   company incorporated for the purpose of the acquisition by its owners,
   the [66]Private Equity investment group [67]Apax and the [68]Guardian
   Media Group in late March/early April.^[69][6]^[70][7]

[[71]edit] Operations

[[72]edit] EMAP Radio

          See also: [73]Bauer Radio

   EMAP operated seven DAB multiplexes and also three jointly owned
   multiplexes with [74]UTV. In addition, [75]EMAP Radio owned 40 [76]UK
   and [77]Ireland local commercial [78]radio stations. On 21 June 2005,
   EMAP acquired 21 radio stations from its purchase of [79]Scottish Radio
   Holdings.

   Twenty of its local contemporary music radio stations based across the
   north of the [80]United Kingdom were marketed as the [81]Big City
   Network.

   In mid-2007, EMAP sold their Republic of Ireland radio interests to
   [82]Denis O'Brien's [83]Communicorp Group Limited (other than
   [84]FM104, which was sold to Communicorp but acquired from Communicorp
   by [85]UTV Media at the same time).

[[86]edit] EMAP magazines

   It has 59 magazines in its business-to-business portfolio including
   Aesthetic Medicine, [87]Architects' Journal, [88]Architectural Review,
   [89]Drapers, [90]Health Service Journal, [91]Local Government
   Chronicle, [92]Nursing Times and [93]Recycling & Waste Management.

[[94]edit] EMAP Middle East

   EMAP Middle East comprises [95]Middle East and [96]North Africa
   ([97]MENA) [98]business-to-business companies [99]AME Info and
   [100]MEED.
