NES Zapper

   NES Zapper in orange

   The NES Zapper, also known as the Beam Gun in Japan,^[11][1] is an
   electronic [12]light gun accessory for the [13]Nintendo Entertainment
   System (NES) and the Japanese [14]Famicom. It was released in Japan for
   the Famicom on February 18, 1984 (1984-02-18) and alongside the launch
   of the NES in North America in October 1985.^[15][1]^[16][2] The
   Famicom version of the Zapper, made for the game [17]Wild Gunman,
   resembled a [18]revolver-style handgun,^[19][1] but the North American
   version resembled a futuristic [20]science fiction ray gun that also
   tied into the design of the NES.^[[21]original research?] Early
   versions of the Zapper were gray, but its color was later changed to
   orange.^[22][3] Although originally included in some configurations of
   the NES, the Zapper was available for purchase separately.^[23][4]

   The Zapper allows players to aim at the television monitor and shoot
   various objects such as ducks, [24]clay pigeons, targets, cowboys, or
   criminals, or other objects. Pulling the trigger emits a loud,
   characteristic noise akin to a combination of a high-tensile spring
   snapping and a mechanical "click".^[[25]citation needed]

[[26]edit] History and usage

   The Zapper was first released in 1985 with the launch of the NES in
   North America. It came bundled with the NES console, the [27]Robotic
   Operating Buddy and two games[28]Duck Hunt and [29]Gyromite.^[30][5]

   The Zapper is used on supported NES games such as Duck Hunt and Wild
   Gunman. When the trigger is pulled, the device sends an invisible beam
   of light at the television screen. If the beam is pointing at an object
   on the screen in sharp contrast to the background, then the game
   registers a hit. To hit targets on the screen, players point the device
   by lining up the front and rear sights with the desired target and
   pulling the trigger. The Zapper could also be used on the title screens
   of games to move the cursordone by pointing the device away from the
   screen and pulling the triggeror starting the game (pointing at the
   screen and pulling the trigger).^[31][6]
