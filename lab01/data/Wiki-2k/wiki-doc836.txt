Edwin Barry Young (pastor)

   Ed Young (Edwin Barry Young, born 16 March, 1961) is the founding and
   senior pastor of [14]Fellowship Church. With four campuses in the
   Dallas/Fort Worth area and one in Miami, Florida, Fellowship is one of
   the most attended churches in North America and was listed as the 3rd
   Most Influential Church in America according to a 2007 survey ^[15][1]

   Young is the oldest son of [16]Homer Edwin Young, senior pastor of
   [17]Second Baptist Church in [18]Houston, Texas. He played basketball
   at Florida State University in [19]Tallahassee, Florida before
   receiving his bachelors degree from [20]Houston Baptist University and
   his [21]Masters of Divinity from [22]Southwestern Baptist Theological
   Seminary in Fort Worth, Texas.

[[29]edit] Fellowship Church

   Fellowship has grown exponentially since its inception in 1990. After
   starting out in a rented office complex, moving to an arts center and
   then meeting in a public high school in [30]Irving, Texas, Fellowship
   Church moved to its current 141-acre campus in [31]Grapevine, Texas in
   the heart of the Dallas/Fort Worth area.
   In 2003 Fellowship Church opened two satellite locations in the
   [32]Plano and [33]Fort Worth areas, with a third satellite opening in
   downtown [34]Dallas just two years later. Fellowship Churchs latest
   satellite campus in [35]Miami opened in May 2006.

   Young is known for the "Sex challenge", in which he urged the married
   couples in his congregation to have sex 7 days in a row. He appeared on
   the January 26, 2009 episode of [36]The Colbert Report to talk about
   this challenge.

[[37]edit] Ed Young Ministries

   [38]Ed Young Ministries is a weekly television program shown throughout
   the U.S and Europe on networks including [39]USA, [40]TBN and [41]CNBC
   Europe.

[[42]edit] Creative Pastors

   Young is a sought-after conference speaker and provides ministry tools
   and life resources through [43]Creative Pastors.^[[44]citation needed]
   Each year, Fellowship Church shares its creative ministry style at the
   [45]Creative Church Conference (C3) with church leaders from all across
   the country during both an international conference and multiple
   regional conferences known as C3 On the Road.
