Ecclesiastical ordinances

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

   Jump to: [6]navigation, [7]search
   [8]Anglicanism portal

   Ecclesiastical ordinances are the [9]bylaws of a [10]Christian
   [11]religious organization, especially that of a [12]diocese or
   [13]province of a [14]church. They are used in the [15]Anglican
   Communion, particularly the American [16]Episcopal Church and [17]Roman
   Catholic Church.

   Ecclesiastical Ordinances is the title of the foundation rules, or
   [18]constitution, of the Reformed Church in [19]Geneva, written by
   [20]John Calvin in [21]1541.^[22][1] ^[23][2] ^[24][3] They were
   revised in [25]1561.^[26][4]

   Under Roman Catholic rules, violators of such ordinances are subject to
   [27]confinement in [28]Ecclesiastical prison.

[[33]edit] Sacrament

   An ecclesiastical ordinance may also be used to describe the
   [34]commandment of [35]Jesus Christ, when he created the [36]sacrament
   of the [37]eucharist, as well as the sacrament of [38]baptism. Such an
   ordinance is generally accepted as a sacrament by both [39]Catholics
   and mainstream [40]Protestants.
