Eastern Sprints

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

   Jump to: [6]navigation, [7]search

   Eastern Sprints refers to the annual rowing championship for the
   [8]Eastern Association of Rowing Colleges (EARC). Since 1974, the
   "Women's Eastern Sprints" has been held as the annual championship for
   the [9]Eastern Association of Women's Rowing Colleges (EAWRC) league.
     * For the women's regatta, see [10]Women's Eastern Sprints

   The [11]Eastern College Athletic Conference (ECAC) sponsors the event
   along with other co-sponsors.

[[25]edit] Participants

   The teams include all of the [26]Ivy League schools as well as others
   such as [27]Georgetown University, Syracuse University, [28]U.S. Naval
   Academy, [29]MIT, [30]BU, [31]Rutgers, [32]Northeastern, [33]Delaware,
   and [34]Wisconsin. In the fall of 2006, George Washington University
   and The College of the Holy Cross were given a two year provisional bid
   to join the league.

   Since 1981, the [35]Quinsigamond Rowing Association (QRA) has invited
   the winner of the Worcester City Championships to compete in the
   varsity eight events at the Eastern Sprints. [36]The College of the
   Holy Cross has secured this invitation since 1996.

[[37]edit] Location

   The race is held at Regatta Point on [38]Lake Quinsigamond in
   [39]Worcester, MA in mid to late May. In general, crews compete in a
   trial heat in the morning, followed by a final (grand, petite, or 3rd
   level) in the afternoon. Each race is a 2000m race including up to six
   crews.

[[40]edit] Team cups

   Since 1946, the Rowe Cup has been awarded to the college whose
   heavyweight men's crews score the highest combined total number of
   points in the varsity, junior varsity, and freshman races. Harvard has
   won the cup the most number of times (27). Princeton comes in second
   with 7 wins.

   Since 1961, the Jope Cup has been awarded to the college whose
   lightweight men's crews score the highest combined total number of
   points in the varsity, junior varsity, and freshman races. Harvard has
   won the cup 19 times. Princeton has won 14 times. The current holders
   are the U.S. Naval Academy, who won it back-to-back 2006 and 2007.

[[41]edit] Course records

   New event record times were set in the 2007 edition of the regatta.
   Harvard now holds the record time in the Varsity Heavyweight event
   (5:27.005) and Dartmouth holds the Varsity Lightweight event record
   (5:38.894).
