Doncols

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

   Jump to: [6]navigation, [7]search

   Doncols ([8]Luxembourgish: Donkels, [9]German: Donkols) is a village in
   the [10]commune of [11]Winseler, in north-western [12]Luxembourg.

[[17]edit] Population

   As of 2001^[18][update], the village has a population of 221.

[[19]edit] Historical background

   Doncols is known as an historically [20]Walloon-speaking village,
   similarly to [21]Sonlez. A. Atten's 'Li leu d' Donc' is considered the
   sole instance of a Walloon language text to have been written by a
   Grand Ducal national [see 'External link', below].

   Even by [22]Luxembourg's trilingual standards, the village is noted for
   the number of different spellings of its name in various languages.
   'Doncols' is its French spelling, 'Donkols' is its German spelling,
   'Donkels' is its Luxembourgish spelling, and 'Donc' is its Wallon
   spelling.
