Eggplant salads and appetizers

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

     (Redirected from [6]Eggplant salad)
   Jump to: [7]navigation, [8]search
   Syrian style [9]Baba ghanoush

   Eggplant salads and appetizers are the [10]salads and [11]appetizers
   made primarily of [12]eggplants ([13]aubergines). There is variety of
   such [14]dishes throughout the cuisines of different regions and
   countries of the world.

[[23]edit] Varieties

[[24]edit] Mediterranean/Middle East

   Baba ghanoush ([25]Arabic bb anj) is a popular [26]Levantine
   ([27]Syrian, [28]Lebanese, [29]Iraqi and [30]Palestinian) dish of
   [31]eggplant (aubergine) mashed and mixed with various seasonings.
   Frequently the eggplant is baked or broiled over an open flame before
   peeling, so that the pulp is soft and has a smoky taste.^[32][1] Baba
   ghanoush is usually eaten as a dip with [33]pita bread, and is
   sometimes added to other dishes. It is usually of an earthy light brown
   color.
   Mutabbel

   Similar to baba ganouj is another Levantine dish [34]mutabbel (), which
   also includes mashed cooked aubergines and [35]tahini, and mixed with
   [36]salt, [37]pepper, [38]olive oil, and [39]anar seeds. Moutabel is
   sometimes said to be a spicier version of [40]baba ghannouj.

   In [41]Armenia the dish is known as mutabal. The essential ingredients
   in Armenian mutabal are eggplant, tahini, garlic, lemon, and onion; and
   most Armenians also add [42]cumin.

   In [43]Turkey, the dish is commonly known as [44]patlcan salatas
   (eggplant salad), also prepared with grilled eggplant, olive oil, lemon
   juice and garlic; sometimes, [45]tahini, chopped tomato and green
   pepper as well. More frequently, eggplant is mixed with yoghurt, olive
   oil and garlic. Patlcan beendi is a similar dish which is served hot
   with meat and it includes cheese, milk and flour.^[46][2]

   In [47]Greece, melitzanosalata is made with [48]olive oil and [49]lemon
   juice.^[50][3]^[51][4]

   [52]Caponata is a [53]Sicilian eggplant relish made from chopped fried
   vegetables (mostly eggplants and peppers), seasoned with celery, olives
   and capers, in a sweet sour sauce. Today, caponata is typically used as
   a side dish or appetizer, but, since the 1700s, it has sometimes been
   used as a main course.

[[54]edit] Eastern Europe

   Salat de vinete spread on slices of bread

   [55]Eggplant salad, Salat de vinete or Vinetta is a both [56]Hungarian
   and [57]Romanian mashed eggplant salad made of grilled, peeled and
   finely chopped eggplants, [58]sunflower oil and chopped [59]onions. The
   eggplants are grilled until they are covered with black ash crust. The
   crust is cleaned off and the remaining cooked eggplant is mashed with a
   blunt, thick wooden knife on a wooden platter (using a metal knife will
   turn the eggplant flesh black). The eggplant mash is mixed in a bowl,
   stirring continuously, with sunflower oil, chopped onions and [60]salt.
   The mix is beaten vigorously. Crushed garlic and ground pepper may be
   added too. Instead of oil, [61]mayonnaise can be used.

   In [62]Bulgaria a typical eggplant appetizer is kyopolou, it is made
   with roasted aubergines and red peppers.

   In [63]Russia and [64]Ukraine, a category of similar dishes is known as
   baklazhannaya ikra ([65]Russian: , literally "eggplant pt" (Note that
   "ikra" in this context means "[66]puree", mashed "[67]ragout" or
   "[68]pt" rather than the [69]homonym "[70]caviar") and some versions
   add chopped [71]tomatoes to the basic recipe.^[72][5] Another eggplant
   salad popular in Russia is called kh'e iz baklazhanov (Russian: , and
   it is probably influenced by [73]Korean cuisine). Eggplant kh'e is
   based on [74]julienned (instead of mashed) cooked aubergines and other
   vegetables, prepared with concentrated vinegar. After adding the
   vinegar, it is set aside for several hours to cure before eating.

[[75]edit] Africa

   In [76]Ethiopia, the eggplant dish is more commonly known as
   blagadoush.

[[77]edit] South Asia

   In [78]Indian cuisine, an eggplant dish, by the name of [79]Baingan
   Bartha, is popular especially in the states of [80]Maharashtra,
   [81]Bihar, [82]Orissa, and [83]West Bengal.The dish has many names,
   depending on the local language ([84]Hindi: baingan ka bharta,
   [85]Bengali: [86]begun bhrta, [87]Marathi: wangyacha bharit). In the
   [88]South Indian state of [89]Tamil Nadu, the [90]Tamils prepare a
   similar dish called kathrikai thayir kothsu, in which the eggplant is
   cooked and mashed and sauted with [91]mustard, red chilis, and sesame
   oil, after which [92]curd is added to the mixture and dressed with
   coriander leaves.
It involves smoked eggplant, mashed with fresh [93]cilantro (coriander leaves),
[94]chili pepper, [95]onion and [96]mustard oil.^[97][6] It is often eaten with
an Indian flatbread (specifically [98]roti or [99]paratha), and is also served w
ith [100]rice, and/or [101]raita (a yoghurt salad).

   Baingan Bartha is also eaten across Pakistan, as well as in the
   neighboring [102]Bangladesh. In [103]Afghanistan a traditional eggplant
   salad is called bonjan salad. It is usually eaten at room temperature
   (or cold) along with main dishes.
