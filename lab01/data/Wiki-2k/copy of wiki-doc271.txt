Asian blepharoplasty

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

     (Redirected from [6]Double eyelid surgery)
   Jump to: [7]navigation, [8]search

   Asian blepharoplasty, also known as "double eyelid surgery," is a type
   of [9]cosmetic surgery where the skin around the [10]eye is reshaped
   ([11]blepharoplasty). The purpose of the procedure is to create an
   upper [12]eyelid with a crease (i.e. "double eyelid") from an eyelid
   without a crease (i.e. "single eyelid", sometimes called "bedroom eyes"
   because of the sleepy look).^[13][1] Anatomically, there are a number
   of differences in the upper eyelids of [14]East Asians compared with
   the differences in the eyelids of other races.^[15][2]

   While there are some Asians with a double eyelid and some without,
   there is also a large variation in the crease position (double eyelid
   size) of the East Asian upper eyelid. The upper lid fold can range from
   1 mm above the eyelash line to about 10 mm. Several methods can be used
   to create the double eyelid--including the full-incisional, partial
   incision and no incision methods (e.g. the DST method). Each has its
   advantages depending on the patient's anatomy and desires.

   Asian blepharoplasties have been reported to be the most common
   aesthetic procedure in [16]Taiwan^[17][3] and other parts of [18]East
   Asia.^[19][4] This procedure has been reported to have some risk of
   complications, but is generally quite safe if done by an expert plastic
   surgeon.^[20][5] Practitioners of Asian double eyelid surgery include
   [21]plastic surgeons, [22]otolaryngologists, and [23]Oral and
   maxillofacial surgeon (facial cosmetic surgeons), and ophthalmologists
   (oculoplastic surgeons). A procedure to remove the [24]epicanthal fold
   (i.e. an [25]epicanthoplasty) is often performed in conjunction with an
   Asian blepharoplasty.^[26][6]
