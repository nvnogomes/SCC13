   The Lockheed EP-3 is the signals reconnaissance version of the [15]P-3
   Orion, operated by the [16]United States Navy. There are 11 EP-3Es in
   the Navy's inventory, the last of which was delivered in 1997. A total
   of 12 P-3C aircraft were converted to replace older versions of the
   aircraft, which had been converted in the late 1960s and early 1970s.
   The aircraft are known by the acronym ARIES, or "Airborne
   Reconnaissance Integrated Electronic System".^[17][1]

   From 1960 to 1991, the squadrons that flew the EP-3E also flew the
   Douglas [18]EA-3B Skywarrior.

[[26]edit] Notable incidents

          Main article: [27]Hainan Island incident

   In April 2001 an aerial collision between a [28]United States Navy
   EP-3E ARIES II, a [29]signals reconnaissance version of the P-3C, and a
   [30]People's Liberation Army Navy [31]J-8IIM fighter resulted in an
   [32]international incident between the United States and [33]China. The
   J-8IIM crashed and its pilot was killed. The EP-3 came close to
   becoming uncontrollable, at one point sustaining a near inverted roll,
   but was able to make an [34]emergency landing on [35]Hainan. The crew
   and plane were subsequently detained by Chinese authorities, accused of
   "killing the Chinese pilot". After several days, the crew was
   repatriated separately to the United States while the aircraft remained
   in China, reported taken apart for research on American technology.
   Although the crew attempted to destroy as much classified material,
   hardware and software on the aircraft prior to the emergency landing,
   there is little doubt that the EP-3 was exploited by Chinese
   intelligence services. An American team was later permitted to enter
   Hainan in order to dismantle the aircraft, which was subsequently
   airlifted back to United States for reassembly and repair.

[[36]edit] Variants

   U.S. Navy Lockheed EP-3A Orion of air test and evaluation squadron VX-1
   Pioneers in 1983. This aircraft was used in the "EMPASS" project, the
   "Electromagnetic Performance of Air and Ship Systems" (EMPASS) Project.
     * EP-3A: Seven modified for electronic reconnaissance testing.
     * EP-3B: Two P-3As converted into [37]ELINT aircraft during the
       Vietnam War.
     * EP-3: ELINT aircraft for the [38]Japanese Maritime Self-Defence
       Force.
     * EP-3E ARIES: 10 P-3As and 2 EP-3Bs converted into ELINT aircraft.
     * EP-3E ARIES II: 12 P-3Cs converted into ELINT aircraft.
     * EP-3J: Two modified from P-3As for [39]FEWSG use as a simulated
       adversary EW platform in exercises; later transferred to the former
       Tactical Electronic Warfare Squadron 33 (VAQ-33), then transferred
       to the former Fleet Air Reconnaissance Squadron 11 (VQ-11).

[[46]edit] Specifications (P-3C Orion)

   Lockheed EP-3E ARIES II in 2006

   General characteristics
     * Crew: 22+
     * Length: 116 ft 7 in (35.57 m)
     * [47]Wingspan: 99 ft 6 in (30.36 m)
     * Height: 33 ft 7 in (10.27 m)
     * Wing area: 1300 ft (120.8 m)
     * [48]Airfoil: NACA 0014-1.10 (Root) - NACA 0012-1.10 (Tip)
     * Empty weight: 77,200 lb (35,000 kg)
     * Loaded weight: 135,000 lb (61,400 kg)
     * Useful load: 57,800 lb (26,400 kg)
     * [49]Max takeoff weight: 142,000 lb (64,400 kg)
     * Powerplant: 4 [50]Allison T56-A-14 [51]turboprop, 4,600 shp (3,450
       kW) each
     * Propellers: Four-bladed [52]Hamilton Standard propeller, 1 per
       engine
          + Propeller diameter: 13 ft 6 in (4.11 m)

   Performance
     * [53]Maximum speed: 311 knots (km/h)
     * [54]Cruise speed: 228 knots (km/h)
     * [55]Range: 2,380 nm (2,739 mi / 4,400 km)
     * [56]Service ceiling: 28,300 feet (8,600 m)
