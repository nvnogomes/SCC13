   Dave Benton (born [13]January 31, [14]1951, real name Efren Eugene
   Benita) is a pop musician and 2001 winner of the [15]Eurovision Song
   Contest.

   Benton was born on the Caribbean island of [16]Aruba in 1951 and in his
   20s moved to the [17]United States. As a drummer and a backing
   vocalist, he worked with [18]The Drifters, [19]Tom Jones, [20]Billy
   Ocean, [21]Jos Feliciano and the [22]Platters.

   While living in the [23]Netherlands in the 1980s, he met his future
   Estonian wife Maris on a cruise ship, then settled in Estonia in 1997.
   He has had a varied musical career in [24]Northern European countries.
   He now lives in Estonia with his wife and two daughters, Sissi and
   Lisa. He performed in the German production of the musical 'City
   Lights' after which he was asked to replace [25]Engelbert Humperdinck
   on his [26]Australian tour.

   Though he has released and produced quite a few albums already, one of
   which in his native tongue, [27]Papiamento, his career to date shows
   him to be more of a performing artist rather than a recording one.

   In 2001 he performed with budding Estonian rock singer [28]Tanel Padar
   and boyband [29]2XL to win the [30]Eurovision Song Contest 2001 with
   their song Everybody.

[[31]edit] Trivia

     * Benton won the [32]Eurovision Song Contest for [33]Estonia but at
       the time, he did not speak [34]Estonian.
     * He is also a grandfather. His oldest daughter from his first
       marriage, Nathaly, gave birth recently to a girl called Tanisia.
