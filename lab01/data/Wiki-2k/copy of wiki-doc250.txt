Dorsal nucleus of vagus nerve

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

     (Redirected from [6]Dorsal motor nucleus of the vagus)
   Jump to: [7]navigation, [8]search
   Brain: Posterior nucleus of vagus nerve
   Nuclei of origin of cranial motor nerves schematically represented;
   lateral view. ("X" visible at bottom center.)
   #9 is vagus nerve and nucleus
   [9]Latin nucleus posterior nervi vagi
   [10]NeuroNames [11]hier-752

   The dorsal nucleus of the vagus nerve (or posterior motor nucleus of
   vagus) is a [12]cranial nerve nucleus for the [13]vagus nerve that
   arises from the floor of the [14]fourth ventricle. It mostly serves
   [15]parasympathetic vagal functions in the [16]heart,
   [17]gastrointestinal tract, [18]lungs, and other [19]thoracic and
   [20]abdominal vagal innervations.

   Section of the medulla oblongata at about the middle of the olive.

   nuclei in red; sensory in blue.
