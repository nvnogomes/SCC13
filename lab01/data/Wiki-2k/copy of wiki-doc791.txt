   San Cristbal Ecatepec de Morelos is a city in the [17]State of Mxico
   and the seat of the municipality of Ecatepec de Morelos; however, both
   the city and the municipality are simply known as "Ecatepec". The name
   "Ecatepec" is derived from [18]Nahuatl, and means "windy hill". It was
   also an alternative name or invocation to [19]Quetzalcoatl.^[20][1]
   "Morelos" is the last name of the hero of the [21]Mexican War of
   Independence ([22]Jos Mara Morelos.)

[[29]edit] The city of San Cristbal Ecatepec

   The city is practically co-extensive with the municipality with the
   city's population of 1,687,549 being 99.9% of the total municipal
   population of 1,688,258.^[30][2]

   "San Cristobl" ([31]Saint Christopher) is the city's patron saint,
   whose feast day is celebrated on [32]July 25 of each year.^[33][1]

   Points of interest include the newest [34]Catholic [35]Cathedral in
   Mexico, [36]Sagrado Corazn de Jess, several colonial era churches and
   the colonal edifice Casa de los virreyes.^[37][1]

[[38]edit] History

   Remains of earliest human inhabitation of the area have been found on
   the nearby Cerro (Hill) de Ecatepec. During the [39]Aztec empire, the
   [40]Mexicas used the town to control trade routes going north.^[41][1]

   Ecatepec was considered an "Repblica de Indios" (Indian Republic)
   [42]1560, allowing the village to maintain a certain amount of autonomy
   and keeping the succession of [43]tlatoani or chiefs. However, in the
   first part of the 17th century, this was changed to a mayorship, with
   the Spanish administrating, along with the communities of [44]Zumpango
   and [45]Xalostoc.^[46][3]

   On [47]October 1, [48]1877, San Cristobl Ecatepec was declared a
   village and "de Morelos" was added to its name.^[49][3]

   The national hero [50]Jos Mara Morelos y Pavn was executed in Ecatepec
   in [51]1815 by the Spanish during the [52]Mexican War of Independence.
   The house in which he was executed is now the Museo Casa de Morelos
   (Museum Hose of Morelos). Ecatepec was declared a city on December 1,
   [53]1980.^[54][1]

   In [55]April 1995, the remains of a [56]mammoth were found in Colonia
   Ejidos de San Cristbal, where the ancient lakes of Xaltocan-Ecatepec
   and Texcoco came together and where the Aztecs build a dam to keep the
   fresh and salty waters separate. The bones have been tentatively dated
   to around 10,500 years B.C.^[57][3]

[[58]edit] The municipality of Ecatepec de Morelos

   As municipal seat, San Cristobl Ecatepec has govening jurisdiction over
   the following communities: [59]San Pedro Xalostc, [60]Santa Clara
   Coatitla (Coatitln), [61]Santa Mara Tulpetlac (Tulpetlac), [62]Santo
   Toms Chiconautla (Chiconautlan), [63]Santo Mara Chiconautla
   (Nepohualco), [64]Guadalupe Victoria (Tecpayocan Coatlalpan), [65]San
   Isidro Atlauhtenco, Banco de Tepetate (La Tepetatera), Coanalco
   Buenavista, El Tejocote, Las Venitas, Tierra Blanca 2a. Seccin (Ejido
   Ecatepec), andVista Hermosa:

   The municipality borders with [66]Tecmac, [67]Nezahualcyotl,
   [68]Acolman, [69]San Salvador Atenco, [70]Tlalnepantla and [71]Distrito
   Federal. The area of this municipality is 155 km (59.85 sq mi).^[72][3]

   Most inhabitants commute to Mexico City for work, and recently the
   [73]Mexico City metro subway system was extended into Ecatepec.

[[74]edit] History

   The area was initially settled by successive waves of [75]Otomis;
   however, because of the later arrival of [76]Toltec-[77]Chichimecas
   that dominated the rest of the [78]Valley of Mexico, this area
   eventually assimilated to the rest of the Valley, ending with its
   domination by the [79]Aztec empire.^[80][3]

   The municipality was officially created on October 13,
   [81]1874.^[82][1]

   Ecatepec de Morelos had a 2005 census population of 1,688,258
   inhabitants, which makes it the most populous municipality in the
   nation [83][1], as well as in the state.
