   Dunkelwerk is the name of a German band which describes its sound as
   Endzeit Electro (dark electronic beats and sounds often combined with
   [18]Nazism themes or horror apocalyptic scenarios and distorted
   vocals), referring to a genre which was originally established by bands
   like [19]:wumpscut: and [20]Lether Strip and is nowadays produced by
   bands like [21]The Retrosic, [22]Suicide Commando or [23]Hocico.
   Dunkelwerk consists of only one person and is a pure studio project,
   not doing any live performances or any other public events. The name of
   the band is an old German idiom which can be translated as "dark work"
   or "dark factory". The producer behind Dunkelwerk has decided to work
   under the pseudonym "losttrooper", being restrained to offer pictures
   of himself.

[[32]edit] History

   Dunkelwerk was signed in 2003 by the Belgian Label [33]Alfa-Matrix. At
   this time the band was a first step towards a harsher direction for the
   label. Dunkelwerk was tipped to Alfa-Matrix by Cyrus of [34]The
   Retrosic (another successful German Endzeit Electro band). Before
   releasing the first album, Dunkelwerk was part of several compilations
   worldwide and released its first single called Die Sechste Armee on the
   [35]Square matrix 004 sampler released by [36]Alfa-Matrix. The war
   theme of the single was a forerunner of the album Troops, which is
   often described as one of the darkest releases in the Endzeit Electro
   genre^[[37]citation needed]. Dealing with German wars, this album
   creates an intense atmosphere of defeat and loss while using danceable
   beats and marching melodies. The combination of dancemusic for clubs
   with such a difficult historical background was one reason why press
   for this album was banned by music magazines like [38]Sonic Seducer in
   Germany (Dunkelwerk was defined as bad entertainment by the editing
   staff in 2005). In subsequent interviews, the producer explained the
   concept of his work and made clear statements about his intention and
   attitude. In the booklet attached to the cd release of Troops, he
   stated: Dunkelwerk is about defeat, not about victory. We have no use
   for any fascist train of thought!

   Troops got positive reviews and gained some success in the worldwide
   industrial scene. It reached position 6 of the German [39]DAC Charts
   and was also released as a limited edition with bonus remixes in a
   special carton box. All tracks on the album are linked to the world
   wars and focus on moments of defeat. The use of rare original broadcast
   recordings from the world wars on many songs distinguishes this band
   from many others. The album was mixed and mastered by Len Lemeire who
   worked with [40]Anne Clark and many other electronic artists.

   Besides the work on the album, the band has made several remixes for
   other bands and offered exclusive versions of its own songs on
   compilations.

   The band has started work on its second album. A first new track was
   released on the sampler [41]Re:connected Vol.2. This new album is
   scheduled to be released in 2009.^[42][1]
