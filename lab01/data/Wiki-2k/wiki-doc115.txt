   The Documentation Center Nazi Party Rallying Grounds ([9]German:
   Dokumentationszentrum Reichsparteitagsgelnde is a museum in
   [10]Nuremberg. It is in the north wing of the unfinished remains of the
   [11]Congress Hall of the former Nazi party rallies. Its permanent
   exhibition "Fascination and Terror" is concerned with the causes,
   connections, and consequences of [12]Nazi Germany. Topics that have a
   direct reference to Nuremberg are especially taken into account.
   Attached to the museum is an education forum.

[[21]edit] Architecture

   In 1994 the city council of Nuremberg decided to establish the
   Documentation Center. The Austrian architect [22]Gnther Domenig
   designed the museum. He won the 1998 international competition with his
   proposal to spear through the northern head of the building with a
   diagonal glass and steel passageway. Inherent in the gesture of this
   project is a pun on the name and a refutation of the chief Nazi
   architect [23]Albert Speer who had directed a masterplan for this site
   including a Zeppelin Field, a stadium to hold 400,000, a March Field
   for military exercises, a Congress Hall for 50,000, and a 180-foot
   (55 m) wide Great Road.^[24][1] This is where Speer had created the
   "cathedral of light" and where the Nazis drew nearly a million people
   in rallies between 1933 an 1938. These were captured on film by
   [25]Leni Riefenstahl in [26]Triumph of the Will. Domenig, the son of a
   Nazi judge, confronted his own personal history in addition to the
   history and [27]Nazi architecture of the project's site.^[28][2] On
   [29]November 4, [30]2001 the project was unveiled by [31]Johannes Rau,
   then [32]President of Germany.

[[33]edit] Exhibition

   The permanent exhibition "Fascination and Terror" (Faszination und
   Gewalt) studies the causes, coherence, and consequences of National
   Socialism. Aspects that have a clear reference to Nuremberg, are
   accentuated because Nuremberg was regarded as the "city of the party
   rally" (Stadt der Reichsparteitage) under the Third Reich, and was used
   many times for propaganda purposes. Among the topics with local
   reference are the history of the [34]Nuremberg Rally, the [35]Nuremberg
   Laws, the [36]Nuremberg Trials against the chief executives of the Nazi
   was criminals from 1945 to 46 and the twelve successive trials, as well
   as the buildings of the [37]Nazi party rally grounds and the contact
   with [38]Nazi architecture after 1945. A wearable "Audioguide" leads
   visitors through the exhibition, in different languages like German,
   French, English, and so on.

[[39]edit] Education forum

   The attached education forum is in the glazed cube on the roof of the
   congress hall. A union of seven Nuremberg educational institutions
   under the management of the museen der stadt nrnberg makes possible an
   extensive and target group oriented program. The offer extends from 45
   minute exhibition tours to several day seminars aimed at school classes
   for both youth and adult groups. The content includes such topics as
   "the power of images", "youth between adaptation and resistance", and
   "the system of concentration camps."

   The partners of the education center are:
     * Geschichte Fr Alle e.V. Institut fr Regionalgeschichte
     * Jugendakademie im Caritas-Pirckheimer-Haus
     * Jugendzentrum fr kulturelle und politische Bildung der Stadt
       Nrnberg
     * Jugendring/Kreisjugendring Nrnberg-Stadt
     * Kunst -und Kulturpdagogisches Zentrum der Museen in Nrnberg
     * Menschenrechtsbro der Stadt Nrnberg
     * Nrnberger Menschenrechtszentrum e.V.

[[40]edit] Awards

   In 2001, the Innovation-prize of the Nuremberg region was awarded to
   the partners of the Documentation Center in the education forum.

   In 2002, the British Guild of Travel Writers awarded the Documentation
   Center with an award for Best New Overseas Tourism Project.^[41][3]

   In 2004, Gnther Domenig was honored by the 9th International
   Architecture Exhibition of the [42]Venice Biennale in their section
   "Transformations." The citation remarked on the opposition of the
   geometry between the project by Domenig versus the existing structure,
   and the resulting creation of a profound memorial.^[43][4]

[[44]edit] Information system

   Since May 2006, the exhibition in the Documentation Center has been
   supplemented by an information system with 23 [45]stele in the grounds,
   that make possible an individual tour of the former Nazi Party Rallying
   Grounds.
