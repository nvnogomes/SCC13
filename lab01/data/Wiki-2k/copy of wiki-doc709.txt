Mississippian

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

     (Redirected from [6]Early Carboniferous)
   Jump to: [7]navigation, [8]search
   For other uses, see [9]Mississippian (disambiguation).

   The Mississippian is an [10]epoch in the [11]geologic timescale or a
   [12]series of the [13]geologic record. It is the earliest/lowermost of
   two divisions of the [14]Carboniferous [15]period lasting from roughly
   359 to 318 [16]Ma (million years ago). As with most other
   [17]geochronologic units, the [18]rock beds that define the
   Mississippian are well identified, but the exact start and end dates
   are uncertain by a few million years. The Mississippian is so named
   because rocks from this age are exposed in the [19]Mississippi River
   valley.

   In [20]North America, where the interval consists primarily of marine
   [21]limestones, it was in the past treated as a full-fledged geologic
   period between the [22]Devonian and the [23]Pennsylvanian. In
   [24]Europe, the Mississippian and Pennsylvanian are one more-or-less
   continuous sequence of lowland continental deposits and are grouped
   together as the [25]Carboniferous [26]system, and sometimes called the
   [27]Upper Carboniferous and [28]Lower Carboniferous instead. During the
   Mississippian Epoch an important phase of [29]orogeny occurred in the
   [30]Appalachian Mountains.

[[31]edit] Subdivisions

   In the official geologic timescale, the Mississippian is subdivided
   into three stages:
     * Tournaisian [32]359.2  2.5 million years ago to [33]345.3
       2.1 million years ago
     * Visean [34]345.3  2.1 million years ago to [35]326.4  1.6 million
       years ago
     * Serpukhovian [36]326.4  1.6 million years ago to [37]318.1
       1.3 million years ago

   The first two come from European stratigraphy, the last is from Russian
   stratigraphy. Besides Europe and Russia, there are many local
   subdivions that are used as alternatives for the international
   timescale. In the North American system, the Mississippian is
   subdivided into four stages:
     * Kinderhookian (the lower two-thirds of the Tournaisian)
     * Osagean (top of the Tournaisian and bottom of the Visean)
     * Meramecian (middle Visean)
     * Chesterian (top of the Visean plus the Serpukhovian)
