   Donald Hiss ([15]December 15, [16]1906 - [17]May 18, [18]1989) was the
   younger brother of [19]Alger Hiss.

[[20]edit] Biography

   Donald Hiss was born on [21]December 15, [22]1906 in [23]Baltimore,
   Maryland. He graduated from [24]Johns Hopkins University and the
   [25]Harvard Law School.^[26][1]

   In 1932 he was a law secretary to Associate Justice [27]Oliver Wendell
   Holmes of the [28]United States Supreme Court. From 1933 to 1935 he was
   employed by the [29]Agricultural Adjustment Administration of the
   [30]United States Department of Labor.^[31][1] In 1934 he was also
   attached to a special Senate committee investigating the munitions
   industry.

   In 1935 he was employed as a special attorney by the [32]United States
   Department of Justice. On September 18, 1936, he was appointed an
   assistant to the Assistant Secretary of State and worked in the
   [33]State Department throughout [34]World War II until 1945.

   Donald, like his brother Alger, was allegedly a secret member of the
   [35]Ware Group, a group of United States government employees who
   belonged to the [36]Communist Party of the United States and engaged in
   espionage for the Soviet Union. This allegation, made early in the
   Alger Hiss case, was never supported and quickly dropped. Donald Hiss
   continued a successful practice with a major Washington law firm until
   retirement and death.

   He died of [37]lung cancer on [38]May 18, [39]1989 in [40]St. Michaels,
   Maryland.^[41][1]
