Duple Dominant

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

     (Redirected from [6]Duple Dominant Bus)
   Jump to: [7]navigation, [8]search
   A Duple Dominant body on Leyland Leopard chassis
   A Duple Dominant IV body on Leyland Tiger chassis

   The Duple Dominant was a design of [9]coach bodywork built by [10]Duple
   between 1972 and 1982. It had an all-steel structure.

[[11]edit] Chassis

   Duple Dominant bodywork was built on different chassis types including:
     * Albion Viking EVK55CL
     * [12]AEC Reliance
     * Bedford YRQ, YRT, YMT
     * Bristol LH
     * DAF MB
     * [13]Ford R1014, R1114
     * [14]Leyland Leopard
     * [15]Leyland Tiger
     * [16]Volvo B58
     * [17]Volvo B10M

     * In 1973 Hutchins Coaches of Pontardawe, Swansea had a new Dominant
       on a [18]Ford R226 chassis, reg no HWN 509L

[[19]edit] Variants

   The original design had a windscreen (and matching rear windows) of the
   same height as the side windows. It remained in production (with a new
   design of grille) alongside the Dominant II, whereafter it was known as
   the Dominant I.

   The Dominant II (introduced in 1976) had a deeper windscreen,
   rectangular headlights and a flat rear window.

   The Dominant III had shallow, [20]parallelogram-shaped side windows.
   The Dominant IV had conventional-shaped side windows, but shallower
   than those on the Dominant II.

   The Duple Goldliner was a high-floor version of the Dominant III or IV.

   The Duple Dominant E was a bus-specification version of the Dominant I
   or II. It is not to be confused with the visually dissimilar Dominant
   Bus which was available from 1974 to 1987.
