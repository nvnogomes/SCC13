   JSC "Domodedovo Airlines" ([22]Russian: ) was an [23]airline based in
   [24]Moscow, [25]Russia. It operated scheduled flights within Russia and
   the [26]CIS, with a focus on flights to the [27]Russian Far East. The
   airline also operated scheduled and [28]ad-hoc charter flights to
   [29]China, [30]Europe, [31]Thailand, [32]Maldives, [33]Malaysia and
   [34]Singapore. It was based at Moscow's [35]Domodedovo International
   Airport.^[[36]citation needed]

[[42]edit] History

   ^[[43]citation needed]

   Domodedovo United Air Detachment ([44]Russian: ), a division of
   [45]Aeroflot Soviet Airlines, was established in March 1964 , when
   Flight Units 206, 211 and 202 were transferred to [46]Domodedovo
   Airport from [47]Vnukovo Airport. The division was set up to operate
   long-haul services within the [48]USSR, particularly to the [49]Russian
   Far East and the [50]Central Asian republics. All flights of the
   airline were operated as part of [51]Aeroflot services.

   On [52]25 March [53]1964 the airline operated its first revenue service
   with a [54]Tupolev Tu-114 on a flight from Domodedovo Airport to
   [55]Khabarovsk in the Russian Far East. In 1978 , with an [56]Ilyushin
   Il-62, the airline started non-stop operations on the longest domestic
   service in the world, Moscow to [57]Petropavlovsk-Kamchatsky, a
   distance of some 6,800km. In 1979 the airline was renamed to Domodedovo
   Civil Aviation Production Association (Domodedovo CAPA) ([58]Russian: (
   )).

   Domodedovo CAPA was officially separated from Aeroflot on [59]20
   January [60]1993, when it was awarded with operator licence 4 by the
   State Civil Aviation Service. In January 1998 , as a result of the
   restructuring of Domodedovo CAPA in which the [61]airport and airline
   were separated, the entity which is today known as Domodedovo Airlines
   was established as Domodedovo Airlines, [62]JSC.

   It was partially privatised in 2001 , with the ministry of property
   retaining a controlling stake^[63][1]. In October [64]2004 [65]KrasAir
   and Domodedovo set up a joint management company called Air Bridge.
   While retaining separate legal identities the airlines are planning to
   integrate their networks and services, which are largely complementary.
   The alliance was renamed [66]AiRUnion in 2005 . Domodedovo Airlines is
   owned by State holding (50.4%) and ATON (49.6%) and has 1,489 employees
   (at March [67]2007)^[68][1].

   It had suspended operations at the end of [69]September 2008 due to the
   [70]Air Union collapse.
