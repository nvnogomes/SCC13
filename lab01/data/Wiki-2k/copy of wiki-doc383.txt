Coloratura soprano

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

     (Redirected from [6]Dramatic Coloratura)
   Jump to: [7]navigation, [8]search

   A coloratura soprano is a type of operatic [9]soprano who specializes
   in music that is distinguished by agile runs and leaps. The term
   [10]coloratura refers to the elaborate ornamentation of a melody, which
   is a typical component of the music written for this voice. Within the
   coloratura category, there are roles written specifically for lighter
   voices known as lyric coloraturas and others for larger voices known as
   dramatic coloraturas. Some roles may be sung by either voice. For
   example, [11]Donizetti's [12]Lucia di Lammermoor was famously done at
   the Metropolitan Opera for many years by Lyric Coloratura [13]Lily
   Pons, whose voice was quite small and light, but more recently the same
   role was sung by [14]Ruth Ann Swenson whose voice is larger. Likewise,
   dramatic coloraturas have been extremely successful in singing the
   lighter roles. Categories within a certain voice range are determined
   by the size, weight and color of the voice.

[[25]edit] Lyric Coloratura Soprano

   A very agile light voice with a high upper extension, capable of fast
   vocal [26]coloratura. Lyric coloraturas have a range of approximately
   middle C (C4) to "high F" (F6). Such a soprano is sometimes referred to
   as a soprano leggiero if her vocal timbre has a slightly warmer
   quality. The soprano leggiero also typically does not go as high as
   other coloraturas, peaking at a "high E" (E6).^[27][1] [28]Bel canto
   roles were typically written for this voice, and a wide variety of
   other composers have also written [29]coloratura parts. [30]Baroque
   music, [31]early music and [32]baroque opera also have many roles for
   this voice.^[33][2]

[[156]edit] Dramatic coloratura soprano

   A coloratura soprano with great flexibility in high-lying velocity
   passages, yet with great sustaining power comparable to that of a full
   spinto or dramatic soprano. Dramatic coloraturas have a range of
   approximately middle C (C4) to "high F" (F6). Various dramatic
   coloratura roles have different vocal demands for the singer - for
   instance, the voice that can sing Abigail (Nabucco, Verdi) is unlikely
   to also sing Lucia (Lucia di Lammermoor, Donizetti), but a factor in
   common is that the voice must be able to convey dramatic intensity as
   well as flexibility. Roles written specifically for this kind of voice
   include the more dramatic [157]Mozart and [158]bel canto female roles
   and early [159]Verdi.^[160][4]

[[246]edit] Sfogato soprano

   In rare instances, some coloratura sopranos are able to sing above high
   F (F6) with great ease. These coloraturas are sometimes referred to as
   sfogato sopranos.^[247][6] Although both lyric and dramatic coloraturas
   can be sfogato sopranos, one of the defining characteristics of the
   Sfogato sound is a light and easy upper extension above F6 making it
   more unusual for a singer with a darker or heavier sound to be
   considered a sfogato soprano.^[248][7] Classic examples of a sfogato
   soprano include [249]Mado Robin, [250]Lily Pons, [251]Toti Dal Monte,
   and [252]Amelita Galli-Curci. Some scholars have speculated that
   [253]Maria Callas was a sfogato soprano. However, this view is not
   widely held as Callas had a darker quality to the voice than what is
   normally considered typical of a sfogato soprano.^[254][8]

[[255]edit] Listen and Compare

   It should also be noted that several of the dramatic soprano roles are
   also the province of lyric coloraturas, and that some dramatic
   coloraturas move easily between the [256]lyric coloratura. Both voices
   can also sometimes sing roles into the [257]lyric soprano, the
   [258]soubrette and rarely the [259]lirico spinto reperetoire.^[260][9]
