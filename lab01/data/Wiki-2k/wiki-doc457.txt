Duchess Cecilie of Mecklenburg-Schwerin

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

   Jump to: [6]navigation, [7]search
   Duchess Cecilie as the Crown Princess of Prussia in 1908

   Duchess Cecilie Auguste Marie of Mecklenburg-Schwerin ([8]20 September
   [9]1886 at [10]Schwerin, [11]Germany[12]6 May [13]1954 at [14]Bad
   Kissingen, [15]Germany) was the wife of [16]German Crown Prince
   William, the son of [17]German Emperor William II.

   She was the daughter of [18]Grand Duke Frederick Francis III of
   Mecklenburg-Schwerin and [19]Grand Duchess Anastasia Mikhailovna of
   Russia.^[20][1]

   Duchess Cecilie married William on [21]6 June [22]1905 in
   [23]Berlin.^[24][1] Once she was married she was styled Her
   [25]Imperial and Royal Highness The German Crown Princess and was also
   Crown Princess of Prussia. She and her husband are buried at
   [26]Hohenzollern Castle

   Their children were:
     * [27]Prince Wilhelm of Prussia (1906-1940)
     * [28]Prince Louis Ferdinand of Prussia (1907-1994)
     * [29]Prince Hubertus of Prussia (1909-1950)
     * [30]Prince Friedrich of Prussia (1911-1966)
     * Princess Alexandrine of Prussia (1915-1980)
     * Princess Cecilie of Prussia (1917-1975)

   [31]Cecilienhof Palace in [32]Potsdam as well as the sailing ship
   [33]Herzogin Cecilie were named after her.
