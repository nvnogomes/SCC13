   Edward L. G. Bowell (or Ted Bowell) (born 1943 in [1736]London) is an
   American [1737]astronomer. Bowell was educated at [1738]Emanuel School
   London, [1739]University College, London, and the [1740]Universite de
   Paris.

   He is principal investigator of the [1741]Lowell Observatory
   Near-Earth-Object Search (LONEOS).

   He has discovered a large number of [1742]asteroids, both as part of
   LONEOS and in his own right before LONEOS began. Among the latter are
   the [1743]Trojan asteroids [1744]2357 Phereclos, [1745]2759 Idomeneus,
   [1746]2797 Teucer, [1747]2920 Automedon, [1748]3564 Talthybius,
   [1749]4057 Demophon, and [1750](4489) 1988 AK.

   He also co-discovered the periodic [1751]comet [1752]140P/Bowell-Skiff,
   and the non-periodic comet [1753]C/1980 E1.
