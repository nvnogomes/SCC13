Dorze

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

   Jump to: [6]navigation, [7]search

   The Dorze are a small [8]ethnic group in [9]Ethiopia who speak a
   language in the [10]Omotic family. Numbering approximately 28,000, they
   live primarily in the southern region of the country, though some have
   migrated to [11]Addis Ababa and other regions. Many Dorze live in
   villages near the cities of [12]Chencha and [13]Arba Minch, which are
   located in the [14]Semien Omo Zone of the [15]Southern Nations,
   Nationalities, and Peoples Region (formerly in the [16]Gamu-Gofa
   province). [17]Weaving is a primary profession for many Dorze.

   Their polyphonic multi-part vocal music features a sophisticated use of
   [18]hocket, showing some similarities with the [19]music of the central
   African pygmies.
