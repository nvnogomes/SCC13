Dunham classification

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

   Jump to: [6]navigation, [7]search

   The Dunham classification system for [8]carbonate [9]sedimentary rocks
   was devised by [10]Kingsley Charles Dunham in 1964, and refined by
   [11]Embry and [12]Klovan in 1971 to include [13]sediments that were
   organically bound during [14]deposition.

   The classification is a way of describing the composition of
   [15]calcareous [16]rocks. For descriptions detailing the textural
   components of sediments and sedimentary rocks, the [17]Folk
   classification is generally preferred - both are equally valid methods
   of classification with different emphases.

[[31]edit] [32]Mudstone

   Contains less than 10% grains (usually assessed by [33]area in cut or
   [34]thin section), supported by a [35]lime [36]mud.

[[37]edit] [38]Wackestone

   Consists of more than 10% grains, supported by a lime mud.

[[39]edit] [40]Floatstone

   Consists of more than 10% large grains (>2 mm), supported by a lime
   mud.

[[41]edit] [42]Packstone

   Contains lime mud, but is grain supported.

[[43]edit] [44]Rudstone

   Coarse [45]limestones supported by grains larger than 2 mm.

[[46]edit] [47]Grainstone

   Lacks mud, and is grain supported.

[[48]edit] [49]Boundstone

   Describes sediment where the original components have been bound
   together after deposition.

[[50]edit] [51]Bafflestone

   [52]Organisms acted as [53]baffles during deposition, reducing the
   local depositional [54]energy. Will contain traces of baffling organism
   and smaller grains that would be expected from the [55]palocurrent
   strength.

[[56]edit] [57]Bindstone

   Organisms (such as [58]algae) encrust the elements during deposition
   and bind them together.

[[59]edit] [60]Framestone

   A [61]solid calcareous or [62]siliceous [63]framework is maintained by
   an organism such as a [64]coral or [65]sponge.

[[66]edit] [67]Crystalline carbonate

   Does not have recognisable depositional structures.

[[68]edit] References

     * Dunham, R. J., 1962, Classification of carbonate rocks according to
       depositional texture, in Ham, W. E. ed., Classification of
       carbonate rocks: [69]American Association of Petroleum Geologists
       Memoir 1, p. 108-121.
