   DRESS syndrome stands for Drug Rash (or Reaction) with Eosinophilia and
   Systemic Symptoms. It is also known as Drug Hypersensitivity Syndrome.
   The symptoms of DRESS syndrome usually begin 1 to 8 weeks after
   exposure to the offending drug. Classic symptoms are [19]exanthem,
   fever and involvement of one or more internal organs. Patients will
   often have fever, malaise, [20]pharyngitis and [21]lymphadenopathy.
   Approximately 50% of patients will have [22]hepatitis, 30% will have
   [23]eosinophilia, 10% will have [24]nephritis and 10% will have
   [25]pneumonitis. Drugs that commonly induce DRESS syndrome include
   [26]phenobarbitol, [27]carbamazepine, [28]phenytoin,^[29][1]
   [30]lamotrigine, [31]minocycline, [32]sulfonamides,
   [33]allopurinol,^[34][2] [35]modafinil and [36]dapsone.
