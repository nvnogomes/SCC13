   Dos Caras Jr. (born May 25, 1977), Dos Caras (meaning "Two Faces" in
   [12]Spanish), is a [13]Mexican [14]professional wrestler in [15]lucha
   libre and a [16]mixed martial artist (MMA).

   Dos comes from a family of professional wrestlers. His father is
   [17]Dos Caras Sr.(making his surname Rodriguez but his name unknown),
   his uncles are Sicodelico and [18]Mil Mscaras, and his cousin
   Sicodelico Jr. They are worldwide superstars as professional
   [19]wrestlers.^[20][1]

[[28]edit] Career

   Born in [29]San Luis Potos, San Luis Potos, [30]Mexico, Dos graduated
   from Universidad Autonoma de San Luis Potosi where he earned a degree
   in [31]architecture.^[32][2]

   Caras made his pro wrestling debut on [33]October 11, [34]2000, teaming
   with his father [35]Dos Caras to defeat [36]El Azteca and Chiba, in an
   event promoted by the [37]Japanese independent promotion [38]Pro
   Wrestling Kageki. This card was held in [39]Shimonoseki.

   Before turning pro, Caras Jr. was a member of the Mexican national team
   in [40]Greco-Roman wrestling for five years, and he placed third at the
   World Junior Championships in 1997 (held in [41]Czech Republic), won
   the [42]Central American and Caribbean Games three times and also won a
   medal at the [43]Pan American Games. He was looking forward to
   competing in the [44]Sydney 2000 Olympics, but due to lack of support
   and political and government problems [45]Mexico could not send any
   representatives to the Olympic wrestling competition, so he turned pro
   instead.^[46][1]

   He made his [47]AAA debut on [48]September 4 when he ran-in during a
   match involving his father, who was getting beat up by Texano,
   [49]Pirata Morgan and [50]Espectro Jr., but his first actual match was
   on [51]September 29, [52]2000, in AAA's "Verano de Escndalo" supershow.
   He teamed with [53]Alebrije, [54]Sangre Chicana and [55]La Parka Jr. to
   beat "Los Consagrados" ([56]Pirata Morgan, [57]Espectro Jr., [58]El
   Texano and [59]El Cobarde).

   During his first months he feuded with the veteran stable of "[60]Los
   Consagrados," and on [61]January 21, [62]2001, he failed (teaming with
   his father) to conquer Texano and Pirata Morgan's National Tag Team
   titles.

   Dos Caras Jr. has been pushed as a top heavyweight tecnico since
   joining [63]CMLL, but he lacks a notable feud at the moment. He's had
   mini feuds with [64]ltimo Guerrero, [65]Universo 2000, [66]Kenzo Suzuki
   and [67]Mark "Marco Corleone" Jindrak, formed a team with [68]Lizmark
   Jr. and [69]Rey Bucanero, and done some work as a trio with [70]Dr.
   Wagner Jr. and [71]Mstico, but really hasn't had a monumental feud.

   Dos has also done some [72]MMA fighting - sometimes with his mask and
   sometimes without - for [73]DEEP and PRIDE. His MMA training included
   training at [74]Marco Ruas gym. He's incorporated submissions to his
   fighting style.^[75][3]

   One of his most memorial matches was against [76]Kengo Watanabe in
   DEEP:2nd Impact when he did one of his trademark wrestling moves the
   [77]German suplex which broke Kengo Watanabe's arm.
