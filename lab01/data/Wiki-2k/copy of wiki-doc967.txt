Edwin Hickman Ewing

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

     (Redirected from [6]Edwin Ewing)
   Jump to: [7]navigation, [8]search

   Edwin Hickman Ewing ([9]December 2, [10]1809 [11]April 24, [12]1902)
   was an [13]American politician and a member of the [14]United States
   House of Representatives for [15]Tennessee's [16]8th congressional
   district.

[[17]edit] Biography

   He was born in [18]Nashville, and after completing preparatory studies,
   he graduated from the [19]University of Nashville in 1827, studied law,
   and was admitted to the [20]bar in 1831. He commenced practice in
   Nashville and became a [21]trustee of the University of Nashville in
   1831, serving until his death. In 1841 and 1842, Hickman was a member
   of the [22]Tennessee House of Representatives.

   He was elected as a [23]Whig to the [24]Twenty-ninth Congress, serving
   from [25]March 4, [26]1845 to [27]March 3, [28]1847, and was not a
   candidate for renomination. He resumed the practice of law in
   Nashville, and after the [29]Civil War, he was appointed president of
   the University of Nashville. He died in [30]Murfreesboro, Tennessee in
   1902, and was [31]interred in Murfreesboro City Cemetery. He was the
   brother of fellow congressman [32]Andrew Ewing.
