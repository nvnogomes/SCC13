Drill line

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

   Jump to: [6]navigation, [7]search

   In a [8]drilling rig, the drill line is a multi-thread, twisted wire
   rope that is threaded or [9]reeved through the [10]traveling block and
   [11]crown block to facilitate the lowering and lifting of the [12]drill
   string into and out of the [13]wellbore.

   On larger diameter lines, tension strengths over a million pounds are
   possible.

   To Make a connection is to add another segment of [14]drill pipe onto
   the top the [15]drill string. This is done by pulling the [16]kelly
   above the rotary table, stopping the mud pump, hanging off the drill
   string in the rotary table, unscrewing the kelly from the drill pipe
   below, swinging the kelly over to permit connecting it to the top of
   the new segment (which had been placed in the [17]mousehole), and then
   screwing this assembly into the top of the existing drill string. Mud
   circulation is resumed and the drill string lowered into the hole until
   the bit takes weight at the bottom of the hole. Drilling ahead then
   resumes.
