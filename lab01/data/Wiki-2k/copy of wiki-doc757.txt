East Point Business Park

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

   Jump to: [6]navigation, [7]search
   Photo taken at East Point Business Park, showing the pond and, from
   right to left, Blocks I, H (both of [8]Oracle) and G ([9]Sun
   Microsystems).
   Photo from the car park between Blocks B ([10]Eircom) and C (Oracle),
   showing block K ([11]Lexmark) in the centre of the image.

   The East Point Business Park ([12]Irish: Gn na Rinne Thoir) in
   [13]Dublin, [14]Ireland is one of the country's largest [15]business
   parks, being the place of employment for thousands of people. It is
   situated in the [16]East Point area on land reclaimed from Dublin Bay
   and the [17]River Tolka estuary. The first phase of the park was opened
   for business around 1996. Major companies based in East Point Business
   Park include among others [18]AOL, [19]Bertelsmann, [20]Lexmark,
   [21]Oracle, [22]Sun Microsystems, Quintiles, CampusIT and [23]IBM.

   [24]Enterprise Ireland has recently leased one of the buildings within
   the business park to serve as its new head office. It is a move that
   will merge its four Dublin offices together at one central location,
   from the end of 2007.

   Conduit call centre has set up premises in this area. One of the main
   areas of Conduit is 11850.

   The main entrance to the business park is on Alfie Byrne Road, with a
   service entrance on East Wall Road, and the business park is on the
   DART line; the nearest station is [25]Clontarf Road.
