   Erythronium dens-canis (common name Dog's tooth violet or Dogtooth
   violet) is a small [20]herbaceous [21]flowering plant in the
   [22]Liliaceae, native to [23]Europe, where it is the only naturally
   occurring species.

   It produces a solitary white, pink or lilac flower at the beginning of
   [24]spring. The petals (growing to approx. 3 cm) are reflexed at the
   top and are yellow tinted at the base. The brown spotted leaves are
   ovate to lanceolate and grow in pairs. The cormous white rootstocks are
   oblong and resemble a dog's tooth in appearance; hence the name.

   It can be found growing in shady and humid [25]undergrowth of mountane
   southern Europe.

   In contrast to most plants with the common name "violet," E. dens-canis
   is not in genus [26]Viola. In areas of North America, several other
   [27]Erythronium species are referred to locally as Dogtooth violet.

[[28]edit] Uses

   It is often grown as an ornamental garden plant.

   The plant is edible, and the leaves may be consumed raw in [29]salad,
   or boiled as a [30]leaf vegetable. The [31]corm is also the source of a
   starch used in making [32]vermicelli [33][1].
