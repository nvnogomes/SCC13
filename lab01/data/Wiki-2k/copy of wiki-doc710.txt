Early conceptions of the Channel Tunnel

   While the [13]Channel Tunnel became a reality in the 1990s, throughout
   the centuries many dreamers and innovators had come up with schemes
   attempting to link [14]England and [15]France (or the [16]Low
   Countries) beneath the [17]English Channel.

   A famous depiction of a paranoid fantasy gripping England in the early
   19th century was captured in a famous print demonstrating the invasion
   plans of England supposedly put in place by [18]Napoleon Bonaparte.
   From the air, the [19]Grande Armee was to fly across the Channel in
   [20]Montgolfier balloons. Barges bearing French soldiers were to cross
   the Channel by water. Another aspect of the fictional "invasion" was
   legions of French forces marching underground -- through a tunnel
   supported by rafters and lit by torches.^[21][1]
