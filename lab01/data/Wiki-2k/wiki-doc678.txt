   Expedia, Inc. ([31]NASDAQ: [32]EXPE) is an [33]American company
   headquartered in [34]Bellevue, Washington that operates the [35]Expedia
   travel website, along with other travel-related properties including
   [36]Hotels.com. The company serves customers in [37]Australia,
   [38]Canada, [39]France, [40]Germany, [41]Italy, [42]Japan, [43]the
   Netherlands, [44]Norway, [45]Spain, [46]Sweden, the [47]United Kingdom,
   the [48]United States, and now [49]India and [50]New Zealand. It is
   part of both the [51]S&P 500 and [52]NASDAQ-100 stock market indices.

[[70]edit] History

   Founded as a division of [71]Microsoft in October 1996,^[72][1] Expedia
   was [73]spun off in 1999, and was later purchased by USA Networks in
   2001^[74][2] (USA changed its name to [75]InterActiveCorp in
   2003^[76][3]). IAC spun off its travel group of businesses under the
   Expedia, Inc. name in August 2005, including Expedia, Expedia Corporate
   Travel (now Egencia), [77]TripAdvisor, [78]Classic Vacations, eLong,
   [79]Hotels.com, and [80]Hotwire.com.^[81][4]

[[104]edit] Locations

   Expedia, Inc. maintains headquarters in Bellevue, Washington (Sunset
   North Campus I-90 corridor). The lease expires in December 2008, when
   the headquarters will move to downtown Bellevue, WA. At the new
   location, Expedia has leased 15 floors (3 to 18) of a 20 floor building
   called Tower 333, currently under construction by Hines. Initially, it
   was widely speculated that Google wanted to occupy most of the
   building, but a crane accident in 2006 caused delay and Google backed
   out of the project. ^[105][5]

[[106]edit] Accolades

   Recently Expedia featured in the most admired Internet companies in the
   United States list, released by Fortune. Expedia ranked 3rd, after IAC
   and Google, and was followed by Amazon.com at 4th place. ^[107][6]
