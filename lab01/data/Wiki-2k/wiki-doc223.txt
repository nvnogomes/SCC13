Doppelsldner

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

     (Redirected from [6]Doppelsoeldner)
   Jump to: [7]navigation, [8]search

   Doppelsldner (Double mercenary) were [9]Landsknechts in 16th-century
   [10]Germany who were prepared to fight in the front line, taking an
   extra risk, in exchange for double payment. The stated ratio was that
   one Landsknecht in four would be a Doppelsldner. A small number of the
   Doppelsldner of each company were usually issued with firearms.

   Likewise, Landsknechts schooled in the use of the [11]Zweihnder
   (two-hander), a massive two-handed sword, were entitled to double pay
   and thus qualified as Doppelsldner. The fencing guild of the
   [12]Brotherhood of St. Mark had the monopoly on the use of the
   Zweihnder after [13]Frederick III, Holy Roman Emperor granted it to
   them in 1487.

   The Zweihnder was allegedly used by the Doppelsldner to break through
   formations of [14]pikemen, especially [15]Swiss pikemen, by either
   being swung to break the ends of the pikes themselves or to knock them
   aside and attack the pikemen directly. The factuality of this tradition
   is disputed, but at least as a legend, it appears to date to at least
   the 17th century.

[[16]edit] Appearances in modern media

   Doppelsldners appear in [17]Ensemble Studio's [18]Age of Empires III as
   a German hand combat and anti cavalry unit. Doppelsldners are portrayed
   quite accurately in the game as expensive and effective frontline
   soldiers.
