Egon von Frstenberg

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

     (Redirected from [6]Egon von Fuerstenberg)

   Egon von Frstenberg or Prince Egon of Frstenberg (June 29, 1946 June
   11, 2004) was a [9]fashion designer.

[[14]edit] Family

   The [15]Frstenberg family is descended from both [16]Stphanie de
   Beauharnais (adoptive daughter and first cousin of the stepchildren and
   also adoptive children of Emperor [17]Napoleon I) and the 18th-century
   English collector, writer, and eccentric [18]William Thomas Beckford.

   Eduard Egon Peter Paul Giovanni Prinz zu Frstenberg was the elder son
   of Prince [19]Tassilo of Frstenberg (1903-1989) and his first wife,
   [20]Clara Agnelli (born 1920), a sister of [21]Fiat's [22]Gianni
   Agnelli. He had a sister, [23]Ira, and a younger brother, Sebastian.
   His stepmother was the Texas oil heiress Dr. Cecil Amelia Hudson, ne
   Blaffer; by her, he had two stepbrothers.

[[24]edit] Life

   Egon von Frstenberg was born at [25]Lausanne in Switzerland. Raised in
   great privilege in [26]Venice, Italy, he was baptized by the future
   [27]Pope John XXIII.

   Frstenberg began his career as a buyer for [28]Macy's, and took night
   classes at the [29]Fashion Institute of Technology.^[30][1] He began
   designing clothes for plus-size women, and later expanded to full
   fashion and ready-to-wear lines, including fragrances, product
   licensing and a [31]haute couture line based out of Rome.

   On July 16, 1969 at Montfort l'Amaury, Yvelines, France, he married the
   Belgium-born [32]Diane Simone Michelle Halfin, daughter of a Holocaust
   survivor. She was Jewish, and the senior Frstenbergs objected to the
   couple's union on that basis. They had two children, Prince Alexandre
   Egon (b. January 25, 1970) and Princess Tatiana Desire (b. February 16,
   1971), and were soon divorced. His wife launched her own fashion house
   at Egon's urging, where she created the iconic wrap dress.

   Diane later married media mogul [33]Barry Diller in 2001. In 1983 Egon
   married a Mississippi native, Lynn Marshall (ca. 1950-).

   Frstenberg wrote two top selling books: The Power Look,^[34][2] a guide
   to fashion and good taste, and The Power Look at Home: Decorating for
   Men,^[35][3] a book on home furnishings. He created an [36]interior
   design firm in 1981.^[37][4] His collection of art included works by
   artist [38]Zachary Selig.^[39][5]

   Egon von Frstenberg died at Spallanzani Hospital in Rome. According to
   the [40]New York Post, Frstenberg's widow stated that he died of
   [41]liver cancer caused by a [42]hepatitis C infection picked up in the
   1970s; other sources suggest that [43]AIDS was the underlying
   cause.^[[44]citation needed] Frstenberg had earlier intimated in a New
   York Magazine article that he was bisexual.^[45][6]
