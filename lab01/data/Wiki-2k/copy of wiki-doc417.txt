   Drosophila bifurca is a species of [19]fruit fly. Males of this species
   are known to have the longest [20]sperm cells of any organism on Earth,
   an astonishing 5.8 cm long when uncoiled.^[21][1] The cells are mostly
   tail, and are delivered to the females in tangled coils. In a male's
   entire life he can only make a few hundred such cells. The other
   members of the genus [22]Drosophila also make very few, giant sperm
   cells, with D. bifurca's being the longest.
