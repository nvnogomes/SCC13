   Falls of Cruachan railway station is a [46]railway station located at
   the foot of [47]Ben Cruachan in [48]Scotland. This station is on the
   [49]Oban branch of the [50]West Highland Line, originally part of the
   [51]Callander and Oban Railway.

[[56]edit] Services

   Services are operated by [57]First ScotRail.

   The station is a [58]request stop, used mainly by [59]hikers during the
   warmer months who walk past the Falls to climb Ben Cruachan. It is open
   only during the summer months, during a period formerly associated with
   the Summer Timetable, prior to the timetable change date moving from
   September to December. Cruachan [60]hydro-electric [61]power station is
   also nearby.

[[62]edit] History

   The station originally opened on 1 October 1893 with a single platform
   on the south side of the line. Although it was closed on 1 November
   1965, it re-opened on 20 June 1988.

[[63]edit] [64]Signalling

   Although Falls of Cruachan station has never had any signalling
   directly associated with it, its platform falls within the four mile
   stretch of railway that is protected by the [65]Pass of Brander stone
   signals.
