ENIAC JTI

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

   Jump to: [6]navigation, [7]search

   The ENIAC Joint Technology Initiative (JTI) is a European
   [8]public-private partnership on [9]nanoelectronics. It was officially
   launched in [10]Brussels on February 22nd 2008. The ENIAC JTI is
   officially called ENIAC and is not to be confused with the [11]ENIAC
   European Technology Platform, with which it cooperates closely. The
   headquarters of the JTI is located in Brussels.

   ENIAC combines for the first time efforts of industry, [12]EU Member
   States (and associated states) and the [13]European Commission in the
   field of nanoelectronics. Although other European initiatives, like the
   [14]European Technology Platforms or the regular calls in the
   [15]Framework Programmes have established cooperation between these
   three actors before, none of them bring all together at the same time
   to implement a focused research programme.

   The ENIAC JTI has the legal form of a [16]Joint Undertaking. Over its
   lifespan of 10 years, the JTI is expected to bring together 3 billion
   euros of which more than half will be provided by industry. The
   association [17]AENEAS represents all industrial partners in the JTI.
   The ENIAC Joint Technology Initiative intends to subsidize strategic
   projects, for which the first proposals will arrive in the first half
   of 2008.

   At the moment of the launch, Member States participating in ENIAC were
   [18]Austria, [19]Belgium, [20]Czech Republic, [21]Estonia, [22]France,
   [23]Germany, [24]Greece, [25]Ireland, [26]Italy, [27]the Netherlands,
   [28]Poland, [29]Portugal, [30]Spain, [31]Sweden, and the [32]United
   Kingdom.
