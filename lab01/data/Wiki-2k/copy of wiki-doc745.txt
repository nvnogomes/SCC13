East Geelvink Bay languages

   The East Geelvink Bay or East Cenderawasih languages are a [11]language
   family of a dozen [12]Papuan languages along the eastern coast of
   [13]Geelvink Bay in Indonesian [14]Papua, which is also known as Sarera
   Bay or Cenderawasih.

   The East Geelvink Bay languages are:

          [15]Turunggare, [16]Barapasi, [17]Bauzi, [18]Anasi (Bapu),
          [19]Burate, [20]Demisa, [21]Kofei, [22]Nisa, [23]Tefaro,
          [24]Woria

[[29]edit] Classification

   A relationship between [30]Yawa, spoken on [31]Yapen Island, and the
   East Geelvink Bay languages was tentatively proposed by [32]C. L.
   Voorhoeve in 1975 in a proposal he called Geelvink Bay. The hypothesis
   was taken up by [33]Stephen Wurm, who developed it as part of an
   initial attempt to classify the [34]Papuan languages; however, the
   relationship would be a distant one, and later linguists such as
   [35]Mark Donohue considered Yawa to be a [36]language isolate.

   Clouse 1997 removed the [37]Lakes Plain languages of the upper
   [38]Mamberamo River in the interior of Papua from [39]Trans-New Guinea,
   where Wrm had placed them, and added them to the Geelvink Bay
   languages. However, in his 2005 classification based on comparative
   evidence from pronouns, [40]Malcolm Ross treats all three groups as
   separate families, with Yawa tentatively placed in an extended [41]West
   Papuan family.

[[42]edit] Pronouns

   The pronouns Ross reconstructs for proto-East Geelvink Bay are,

          I    *e we   *i
          thou *o you  *u
          s/he *a they  ?
