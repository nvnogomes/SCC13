   Val-d'Or is a [32]city in [33]Quebec, [34]Canada with a population of
   31,123 in the [35]Canada 2006 Census. It is located in the
   [36]Abitibi-Tmiscamingue [37]region near [38]La Vrendrye Wildlife
   Reserve.

[[46]edit] History

   [47]Gold was discovered in the area in 1923. The name of the town is
   French for "Valley of Gold." While gold is still mined in the area
   today, base metals, such as [48]copper (Cu), [49]zinc (Zn), and
   [50]lead (Pb) have become increasingly important resources. The ore is
   usually found in volcanic rocks that were deposited on the seafloor
   over 2.7 billion years ago. They are referred to as volcanic-hosted (or
   volcanogenic) massive sulphide deposits ([51]VMS).

   The city is known for its vast parks, cycle tracks, and forests. Some
   other attractions include the [52]City of Gold and the mining village
   of [53]Bourlamaque, which were officially proclaimed historic sites in
   1979.

   The city hosted the [54]Quebec Games in 1987. The local hockey team,
   the [55]Val-d'Or Foreurs, have played in the [56]QMJHL since 1993,
   winning the league championship in 1998 and 2001 to claim a spot in the
   [57]Memorial Cup. They play at [58]Centre Air Creebec. The Foreurs'
   mascot is called Dynamit, named after [59]dynamite which was
   extensively used by the mining industry of Val-d'Or.

   Val-d'Or was once home to [60]CFS Val-d'Or, a [61]Canadian Forces
   Station.

   In the [62]municipal reorganizations of [63]January 1, [64]2002,
   Val-d'Or was merged with the neighbouring municipalities of Dubuisson,
   Sullivan, Val-Senneville and Vassan.

[[68]edit] Media

          Main article: [69]Media in Abitibi-Tmiscamingue

   Almost all media in Val-d'Or and the nearby city of [70]Rouyn-Noranda
   serves both cities. Although the cities are far enough apart that radio
   and television stations in the area serve the cities from separate
   transmitters, almost every broadcast station in either city has a
   [71]rebroadcaster in the other city. The only nominal exceptions are
   the cities' separate [72]nergie stations, although at present even
   these stations share the majority of their broadcast schedule.
