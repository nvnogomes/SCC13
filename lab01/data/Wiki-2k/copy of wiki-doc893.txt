   [33]Gen.bryg. Edmund Stanisaw Knoll-Kownacki (1891 1953) was a Polish
   military officer and a high-ranking commander of the [34]Polish Army.

[[42]edit] Youth

   Son of Kazimierz and Maria von Eynatten. After his [43]matura exam in
   1908 in [44]Kaluga, he continued his education at the Department of
   Natural Sciences of the [45]Moscow State University. After five
   semesters he was transferred to the [46]Moscow Agricultural Institute.
   In the course of his studies he was subjected to compulsory military
   service for 12 months. He entered the army in September 1912 at the
   19th Battery of Horse Artillery in Dubno, after which he passed his
   officers exam, earning the rank of reserve warrant officer.

   In May 1913 he received his diploma in agricultural engineering. He
   worked for a year as a veterinary inspector in the Central Agriculture
   Association in Warsaw. Meanwhile, he entered the Rifleman Squads, with
   the nickname Kownacki. He graduated from the Rifleman Squad School in
   [47]Nowy Scz in 1914.

[[48]edit] World War I

   On [49]August 6, 1914, at the beginning of [50]World War I, he became a
   commissioned troop commander in the 2nd Rifleman Battalion. From [51]13
   September he was a member of 1st [52]Uhlan [53]Squadron of [54]Wadysawa
   Beliny-Pramowskiego. From October 1914 he organised and commanded a
   battery of cavalry (part of 1st artillery regiment), later upgraded to
   a wing. After the Oath crisis of [55]August 6, [56]1917 he was interned
   in a camp at [57]Beniaminw. Granted temporary leave in May 1918 to deal
   with family matters, he never returned to the camp. On [58]October 25
   he was appointed to [59]Polska Sia Zbrojna (Polish Military Force) and
   assigned to Headquarters of Artillery Inspector. In November he
   organized the 1st regiment of Legion's field artillery which he
   commanded on the eastern front.

[[60]edit] Polish-Bolshevik War

   [61]September 1, [62]1919 1921.

[[63]edit] Interbellum

   Graduated in December 1924 with a General Headquarters Officer diploma.

[[64]edit] World War II

   In the battle since 1939; became a prisoner of war and was in detention
   through 1945 (since [65]27 April [66]1942 in [67]Oflag VII-A Murnau).

[[68]edit] After the war

   Freed from imprisonment in 1945, lived in [69]France. After arriving in
   Great Britain and demobilizing, he settled on a small farm in
   [70]Wales.

   He died on [71]September 2, [72]1953 and was buried at the Beaumaris
   cemetery in [73]Llangefni, Wales. He was married to Janina Chramiec,
   and had a daughter, Maria Barbara.
