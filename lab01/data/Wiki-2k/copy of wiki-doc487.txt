Dukes Creek

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

     (Redirected from [6]Dukes Creek Falls)

   Dukes Creek Falls
   Map of waterfalls near Helen, Georgia

   Dukes Creek is the creek in [9]White County, Georgia on which gold was
   found in [10]1828. Either Frank Logan or one of his slaves is typically
   given the credit for this find. The discovery of gold in White County
   and neighboring Lumpkin County lead to the [11]Georgia Gold Rush. Dukes
   Creek is a [12]tributary of the [13]Chattahoochee River.

   The 150 foot Dukes Creek Falls, which are actually located on Davis
   Creek at its [14]confluence with Dukes Creek, are accessed by a hiking
   trail called the Dukes Creek Trail. There is an observation platform at
   the falls. Dukes Creek Falls is one of four [15]waterfalls in the
   [16]Chattahoochee National Forest near [17]Helen, Georgia that are
   popular destinations. Two of the other waterfalls, [18]Anna Ruby Falls
   and [19]Raven Cliff Falls, are also in White County and the third
   waterfall, [20]DeSoto Falls, is in neighboring [21]Lumpkin County.

   The Smithgall Woods Dukes Creek Conservation Area also includes a
   portion of Dukes Creek.
