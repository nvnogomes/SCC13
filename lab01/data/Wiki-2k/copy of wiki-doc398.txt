   The Dresdner Philharmoniker (Dresden Philharmonic) is a [17]symphony
   orchestra based in [18]Dresden, [19]Germany. The orchestra was founded
   in 1870 and gave its first concert in the Gewerbehaussaal on 29
   November 1870, under the name Gewerbehausorchester. The orchestra
   acquired its current name in 1915. It receives financial support from
   the city of Dresden.

   One of the orchestra's later concert venues was the Kulturpalast,
   during the existence of the [20]DDR. After German reunification, plans
   had been proposed for a new concert hall. These had not come to
   fruition by the time of the principal conductorship of [21]Marek
   Janowski, who cited this lack of development of a new hall for the
   orchestra as the reason for his resignation from the post in 2004. In
   addition to concerts at the Kulturpalast, the orchestra also performs
   at the Kreuzkirche, the Hochschule fr Musik Dresden, the Schloss
   Albrechtsberg.

   The choral ensembles affiliated with the orchestra are the Dresden
   Philharmonic Choir and Dresden Philharmonic Chamber Choir.
