   Dixie Carter Salinas^[15][2] is an [16]American [17]businesswoman,
   currently serving as [18]president of the [19]limited liability company
   [20]TNA Entertainment. Based in [21]Nashville, Tennessee, TNA
   Entertainment produces [22]professional wrestling [23]television
   programs, live events, [24]pay-per-views and associated merchandise
   such as [25]video games. ^[26][3]

[[31]edit] Career

   Carter was born in [32]Dallas, Texas. She graduated from [33]The
   Hockaday School in 1982 and subsequently attended the [34]University of
   Mississippi, graduating in 1986 with a [35]Bachelor of Business
   Administration. While a student, Carter was active on the Student
   Programming Board, and was a member of the [36]Kappa Kappa Gamma
   [37]women's fraternity. In addition, Carter worked as an [38]intern
   with Levenson and Hill, a marketing and advertisement firm in the
   [39]Las Colinas suburb of [40]Dallas, Texas. Upon graduating, Carter
   became a full-time employee of Levenson and Hill, receiving a promotion
   to the position of vice president at the age of 27. In 1993, she
   started her own business in Nashville, focusing primarily on sport and
   music representation.^[41][3]^[42][4]^[43][1]^[44][5]

   In 2002, the president of Monterey Peninsula Talent (a booking agency)
   contacted Carter and informed her that [45]Total Nonstop Action
   Wrestling, a fledgling professional wrestling promotion, required a
   marketing and publicity outlet. Carter began working with TNA, but, two
   months later, was informed by [46]Jeff Jarrett (a part-owner of TNA)
   that a key financial backer ([47]HealthSouth Corporation, which was
   having its own financial problems due to its being investigated for
   accounting irregularities) had withdrawn support from TNA, and that the
   company was in dire straits as a result.^[48][3]

   Carter, claiming to "[See] the potential in a marketplace that had one
   company with a $900 million [49]USD market cap and no competitor",
   contacted her parents, the owners of [50]Panda Energy International, a
   Dallas-based [51]energy [52]company. In October 2002, Panda Energy
   purchased 71[53]% of TNA from the HealthSouth Corporation for $250,000
   USD. On [54]October 31, [55]2002, TNA (which originally traded as "J
   Sports and Entertainment") was renamed "TNA Entertainment", and Carter
   was appointed president.^[56][6]^[57][3]^[58][4]

   Carter was instrumental in securing a television deal with [59]Fox
   Sports Net in 2004, and in July 2005 she announced that she and several
   other TNA employees, including vice-president [60]Jeff Jarrett, had
   negotiated a more lucrative television deal with [61]Spike TV. Carter
   brought a bottle of [62]champagne to the meeting in order to celebrate.
   Her [63]creative influence on TNA [64]storylines has been less
   publicized, although she is known to be a fan of [65]Jonny Fairplay, a
   [66]reality television contestant who has worked for TNA in the past as
   a [67]manager. She has also expressed admiration for the work of
   [68]Vince Russo, a controversial [69]booker. In [70]September 2006,
   Carter personally negotiated a contract with wrestler [71]Kurt
   Angle.^[72][7]^[73][8]^[74][9]

   Carter has ruled out any possibility of becoming an on-screen
   character, as is the case with [75]World Wrestling Entertainment
   chairman [76]Vince McMahon, and previously with former [77]World
   Championship Wrestling president [78]Eric Bischoff and the
   aforementioned [79]Vince Russo, and former [80]Extreme Championship
   Wrestling owner [81]Paul Heyman. All three individuals eventually
   became prominent on-screen characters, albeit Heyman was already one
   before ECW was formed.^[82][6]

[[83]edit] Personal life

   Carter is married to Serg Salinas, with whom she has two
   children.^[84][8]
