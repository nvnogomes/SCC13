Douglas Dollarhide

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

   Jump to: [6]navigation, [7]search

   Douglas Dollarhide ([8]March 11, [9]1923 [10]June 28, [11]2008) was the
   first [12]African American mayor of a metropolitan city in
   [13]California.

[[14]edit] Biography

   Douglas F. Dollarhide witnessed first hand the transformation of
   [15]Compton, California from a predominantly white Los Angeles suburb
   into one of the most heavily concentrated [16]African American
   communities in the [17]United States. He was elected to the City
   Council in 1963, becoming its first [18]African American member.

   In 1969, Dollarhide made history again, when he became not only the
   first black mayor of [19]Compton, but the first [20]African American
   mayor of a major town in [21]California since American rule. After his
   election crime rose and property values declined throughout Compton.

   Compton's Dollarhide Neighborhood Center is named after him.

   Dollarhide died on [22]June 28, [23]2008, aged 85, at his home in
   [24]Northridge in the [25]San Fernando Valley.^[26][1]
