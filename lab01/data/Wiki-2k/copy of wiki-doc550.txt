Dustin Lee Abraham

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

     (Redirected from [6]Dustin Abraham)
   Jump to: [7]navigation, [8]search

   Dustin Lee Abraham (born in [9]Las Vegas) is an [10]American [11]actor,
   producer and [12]screenwriter. He penned the 2001 cult movie [13]How
   High for [14]Method Man and [15]Redman. He currently produces and
   writes for [16]CSI: Crime Scene Investigation.

   On [17]March 27, [18]2007, Redman confirmed on [19]BET [20]Rap City
   that [21]How High 2, the sequel to How High, is being written by
   Abraham. The script is still in progress though the project is set to
   begin production in August 2008.

   On [22]December 6th, [23]2007, legendary film director [24]William
   Friedkin directed an episode of [25]CSI: Crime Scene Investigation,
   [26]Cockroaches, written and produced by Abraham. This was the first
   time Friedkin had made a presence in television in nearly a decade.

   Abraham has graduated from [27]Chaparral High School in Las Vegas,
   [28]Arizona State University and [29]California State University, Los
   Angeles.

   Before his work in film and television, Abraham won the distinguished
   title of [30]American Forensic Association Dramatic Interpretation
   National Speech Champion. Actor [31]James Dean won the same title when
   he was in high school.

   Was mentored by [32]Anthony E. Zuiker, creator of all three CSI
   franchises, while a student in high school.

   Likes to collaborate with [33]Method Man. [34]How High and character
   [35]Drops on [36]CSI: Crime Scene Investigation.
