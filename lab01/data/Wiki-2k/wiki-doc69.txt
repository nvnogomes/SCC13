   Dniprodzerzhynsk ([24]Ukrainian: , [25]Russian: , Dneprodzerzhinsk) is
   a city in the [26]Dnipropetrovsk Oblast of [27]Ukraine, and a port on
   the [28]Dnieper River. First written evidence of settlement on the
   territory of Dniprodzerzhynsk appeared in 1750. At that time Romankovo
   and Kamianske (Russian: "Kamenskoe") villages, which make the modern
   city, were a part of Nova (New) [29]Sich of [30]Zaporizhian
   [31]cossacks. The city was known as Kamianske until 1936 when it was
   renamed in honor of [32]Felix Dzerzhynsky, the founder of the
   [33]Bolshevik [34]secret police, the [35]Cheka. According to the latest
   data, its population is 273,700.

   A [36]Soviet leader, [37]Leonid Brezhnev was born and raised in
   Dniprodzerzhynsk.

   On 2 July 1996 a notorious traffic [38]accident happened in
   Dniprodzerzhynsk. An overcrowded [39]tram that was moving along a steep
   hill on Chapaeva Street began to rapidly slide downhill (because of the
   brakes failure), eventually derailing and running into a
   school.^[40][2] A total of some 30 people died and more than a 100 were
   injured as a result of that accident.^[41][3] Following a government
   inquiry into the causes of the accident, the then mayor, [42]Sergiy
   Shershnev and his [43]deputy [44]Ihor Laktionov resigned.^[45][2]
   ^[46][4] That accident has had a significant effect on the worldwide
   discussion regarding use of trams in the hilly localities^[[47]citation
   needed].

[[52]edit] Economy

   Dniprodzerzhynsk economic base is almost exclusively centered in
   [53]heavy industry, with ferrous [54]metallurgy being the backbone of
   local economy. Around 57% of the total [55]industrial production is
   comprised of metallurgy and metal working. [56]Chemical industry comes
   second with ca. 17% share of the total industrial output.^[57][5] While
   the exceedingly industrialized nature of the local economy ensures a
   rather high employment rate (as of 01.11.2007, official unemployment
   stood at 1,40%)^[58][1], it also contributes to excessive pollution and
   radiation levels in the city.^[59][6]

[[60]edit] Social life

   Several [61]Eastern Orthodox churches, the largest being the Orthodox
   Church of [62]Saint Nicholas which dates from 1894^[63][7] serve the
   faithful of the city. By 2008 information, there were 14 parishes of
   [64]Ukrainian Orthodox Church in Dniprodzerzhynsk^[65][8].

   The [66]Roman Catholic Church of [67]Saint Nicholas^[68][9] built by
   the city's Polish community at the end of the nineteenth century, has
   become one of the centers of Roman Catholicism in Eastern Ukraine.
   Catholic Parish of Saint Nicholas also includes a monastery run by the
   [69]Order of Friars Minor Capuchin^[70][9].

   The town has an active Jewish community with a new synagogue and
   community center.^[71][10].

   A large dam on the Dnieper river was completed in 1964 built by
   thousands of immigrant workers^[[72]citation needed]. Because of these
   workers, a restaurant culture unique in Ukraine has
   emerged^[[73]citation needed]. A variety of diverse Asian, African, and
   European influences continue to dominate the food scene in the
   region.^[[74]citation needed]
