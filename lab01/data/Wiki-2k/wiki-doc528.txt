   Durango (pronounced [38][duago]) is one of the constituent [39]states
   of Mexico. Its population is 1,509,118.^[40][1] It has Mexico's
   second-lowest population density, after [41]Baja California Sur. The
   city of [42]Durango is the state's capital.

[[51]edit] Geography

   The "estado" of Durango is bordered to the north by [52]Chihuahua, to
   the north-east by [53]Coahuila, to the south-east by [54]Zacatecas, to
   the south-west by [55]Nayarit, and to the west by [56]Sinaloa. Most of
   the state is heavily mountainous and a good part forested, the
   [57]Sierra Madre Occidental occupies the western and central part of
   the state. This mountain range contains a good supply of minerals,
   including the silver that encouraged Spanish occupation of the
   territory after it was discovered. These mines extend north into
   Chihuahua and south into the state of [58]Zacatecas. Vast desert basins
   in the Laguna District are irrigated by the [59]Nazas River.

   Major crops grown in the area include [60]cotton, [61]wheat, [62]corn,
   [63]alfalfa, [64]marijuana, [65]beans, [66]sorghum, and other
   vegetables.

   Durango is famous for its scorpions. Mexicans generally refer to the
   people of Durango as Alacrn de Durango (A scorpion from Durango). The
   demonym for the natives of Durango is Duranguenses.

   The major occupations in Durango are farming, lumbering and
   [67]ranching.

[[68]edit] History

   [69]Francisco de Ibarra, the first to colonize Durango, settled this
   part of the vast northern province of [70]Nueva Vizcaya in 1563, when
   he founded the capital city and named it Durango for the basque town
   [71]Durango, Biscay, [72]Spain, [73]Europe. The foundation was just one
   in his larger program of initiating settlements throughout the
   province.

   This was a late colonization for the Spanish, due mostly to heavy
   resistance by the indigenous population. From first contact to modern
   times, the indigenous peoples have attempted to gain some autonomy,
   address grievances, and maintain traditional land ownership.

   Spanish colonists became highly attracted to the Durango area for its
   mining and grazing prospects.

   In 1823, shortly after victory over Spain in the [74]Mexican War of
   Independence, Durango earned the right to become a separate state.

[[89]edit] Popular culture

   [90]Lists of miscellaneous information should be avoided. Please
   [91]relocate any relevant information into appropriate sections or
   articles. (June 2008)
     * [92]Bob Dylan co-wrote a song called "Romance in Durango" with
       [93]Jacques Levy for his 1976 album [94]Desire (album).

     * In [95]Stanley Kubrick's [96]A Clockwork Orange, the sports car
       driven by Alexander deLarge and his [97]droogies is called a
       "Durango 95".^[98][2]^[99][3]

     * Punk rock band [100]The Ramones often opened their live act with a
       song called "Durango 95".

     * In the song Never Gonna Stop, [101]Rob Zombie sings of the "Durango
       Number 95"

     * Actor [102]John Candy suffered a fatal heart attack in Durango,
       while filming his final motion picture [103]Wagons East.

     * In the book [104]The House of the Scorpion, Mateo Alacran (referred
       to as "El Patrn"), a drug lord, was born in Durango, as was Celia.
       The last name he adapted, "Alacran", is a reference to the term
       Alacran de Durango (Durango Scorpion).

     * The film [105]Dragonball Evolution by James Wong was filmed in this
       state^[106][4].

   The 1948 film The Treasure of the Sierra Madre, starring Humphrey
   Bogart, Walter Huston, and Tim Holt, was set in the state of Durango.
     * In the 1970 film [107]Moonfire, the main storyline is set here.

     * In the song Spanglish by [108]The Game (rapper) he refers to the
       state in the end of his first verse by saying "Get it from Durango,
       take it to Chicago"
