   Donald Blessing Rice (born [16]Frederick, Maryland, June 4, 1939) is a
   [17]California businessman and senior government official. He has been
   [18]president and [19]chief executive officer of several large
   companies including [20]RAND Corporation, and has sat on numerous
   boards of directors, including [21]Wells Fargo & Company. Rice also
   served as the seventeenth [22]Secretary of the Air Force, 1989-93.

[[29]edit] Early life

   Rice was born in 1939 in [30]Frederick, Maryland, the son of Donald B.
   Rice, Sr., and Mary C. Rice. His father operated a service station, and
   later started a tire business, eventually becoming mayor of Frederick,
   while his mother kept the books for the family business. Both parents
   stressed the need for a good education. Business, public service and
   education were all important influences on Rice as he grew up.^[31][1]

   He earned a BS degree in chemical engineering from the [32]University
   of Notre Dame and was commissioned a second lieutenant in the United
   States Army Ordnance Corps upon graduation in June 1961. On August 25,
   1962, Rice married Susan Fitzgerald. The couple are the parents of
   three sons, Donald B. Rice III, Joseph J. Rice, and Matthew F. Rice.

[[33]edit] Public service

   From 1965 to 1967, he served on active duty as a lieutenant and then
   captain of ordnance. During that time, he was assistant professor of
   management and acting deputy director for academics at the Navy
   Management Systems Center in the [34]Naval Postgraduate School at
   [35]Monterey, California. While on active duty, he earned a master's
   degree in industrial management from [36]Purdue University in 1962, and
   then a [37]doctorate in economics in 1965 also from Purdue.^[38][2]

   In 1967, Rice became director of cost analysis in the [39]Office of the
   Secretary of Defense in [40]Washington, D.C. Two years later, he was
   appointed as deputy assistant secretary of defense for resource
   analysis where he was responsible for cost analysis, manpower and
   logistics requirements, and budget planning for defense programs. From
   1970 to 1972, he served as assistant director in the [41]Office of
   Management and Budget. In this position, he managed Federal Government
   budget activities for agriculture, atomic energy, commerce, energy,
   environment, natural resources, public works, science and technology,
   space, and transportation.^[42][2]

   In 1972, Rice became president and chief executive officer of RAND
   Corporation, an independent, nonprofit, public service [43]think tank
   in [44]Santa Monica, California. RAND conducts research and strategic
   analysis on national security issues and offers doctoral degree
   programs in several fields of public policy. During this time, Rice
   also serve as chairman of the National Commission on Supplies and
   Shortages and two terms on the [45]National Science Board. At the
   request of President [46]Ronald Reagan, he directed a comprehensive
   study of the resources management process within the [47]United States
   Department of Defense.^[48][2]

   He remained at RAND until May 1989 when President [49]George H. W. Bush
   appointed him Secretary of the Air Force. As the Air Force Secretary,
   Rice reorganized the [50]Air Force and reduces its budget. The first
   [51]Gulf War, also known as "Desert Storm", was fought while he was
   Secretary. Rice served for four years, from May 22, 1989, until he left
   the Department of the Air Force on January 20, 1993.^[52][3]

   Rice has been president of the Institute of Management Sciences, a
   member of the [53]American Association for the Advancement of Science,
   the American Economic Association and the United States Commission on
   National Security in the 21st Century, and a fellow of the National
   Academy of Public Administration.

   Active in Republican politics, he has been a member of McCain 2000,
   [54]George W. Bush for President, Bush-Cheney '04, [55]John McCain
   2008, the [56]National Republican Congressional Committee, and the
   [57]National Republican Senatorial Committee.

[[58]edit] Businessman

   After he left the Air Force, Rice became president and chief operating
   officer of [59]Teledyne in March 1993, serving until August 1996. He
   left Teledyne to become president and chief executive officer of
   Agensys Corporation, a Santa Monica based [60]biotechnology company. In
   2002, he became chairman of the Agensys board of directors.^[61][4]

   Rice has served on numerous corporate boards. These include [62]Chevron
   Corporation, [63]Johnson & Johnson, Pacific Enterprises, Scios,
   Teledyne, [64]Wells Fargo & Company, [65]Unocal, [66]Amgen, and
   [67]Vulcan Materials Company. He was chairman of the board of directors
   of Scios from 1998 until its sale to Johnson & Johnson in 2003. Rice is
   a member of RAND board of trustees, and chairman of the board of
   governors for RANDs Pardee Graduate School. He is also a member of the
   equipment advisory committee for the PGA Tour.

[[68]edit] Awards

   Rice has received five honorary doctorates degrees. His first honorary
   degree was a doctorate in Engineering from Notre Dame in 1975; his
   second, in Management, was from Purdue in 1985; he received a third, in
   Laws, from [69]Pepperdine in 1989; the fourth, was in Humane Letters,
   from West Coast University in 1993; and the most recent, in Public
   Policy, from the RAND graduate school in 1995.^[70][4]

   In 1999, Rice received the Wanner Award from the [71]Military
   Operations Research Society. Other awards include the Citation Award
   from the [72]University of California at Los Angeles School of
   Engineering and Applied Sciences, the [73]Air Force Associations
   Symington Award, and the [74]Jaycees Ten Outstanding Young Men
   recognition. He is a [75]Ford Foundation doctoral fellow. Rice was also
   awarded [76]Distinguished Civilian Service Medal and the
   [77]Meritorious Service Medal from the Secretary of the
   Defense.^[78][4]
