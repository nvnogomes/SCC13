   Duckhorn Vineyards is a boutique [21]winery in [22]St. Helena,
   [23]California, specializing in premium [24]Merlot wines.

[[29]edit] History

   Founder Dan Duckhorn, a [30]Santa Rosa native and [31]Berkeley
   graduate, toured the [32]Pomerol and [33]Saint-milion in the
   [34]Bordeaux region of [35]France in the mid-1970s with [36]Ric
   Forman,^[37][1] who as [38]winemaker of [39]Sterling Vineyards had
   introduced vintage-dated [40]Merlot wine to California. He returned to
   California with his then-wife Margaret (the couple divorced in 2000) to
   promote a new market for Merlot. They founded Duckhorn Vineyards in
   1976.^[41][1]

   Production grew from 1,600 cases of [42]Cabernet Sauvignon and Merlot
   in 1978 to more than 20,000 cases in 2006. Beginning in 1988 Duckhorn
   purchased seven estate [43]vineyards on [44]Howell Mountain and the
   valley floor,^[45][2] for a total of 168 acres (68.0 ha) in Napa
   Valley, and four vineyards totallying 153 acres (61.9 ha) in the
   [46]Anderson Valley, [47]Mendocino County.^[48][1]

   In July, 2007, [49]GI Partners, a [50]private equity firm, purchased a
   [51]controlling interest in Duckhorn Wine Company and with it
   Duckhorn's various brands and operations^[52][3] at a valuation
   believed to exceed $250 million.^[53][1]^[54][4]

[[55]edit] Production

   Duckhorn Vineyards produces several single-vineyard Merlots and
   Cabernet Sauvignons, as well as a highly regarded but less expensive
   [56]Sauvignon blanc. In 2005, the [57]San Francisco Chronicle
   proclaimed Dan Duckhorn its "Winemaker of the Year" and named four of
   his wines to its prestigious list of "Top 100 Wines of 2005." In
   addition to Duckhorn, Duckhorn Wine Company also operates Goldeneye, a
   maker of [58]Pinot Noir in Anderson Valley, and Paraduxx, a blend of
   [59]Zinfandel and Cabernet Sauvignon made at a winery on the Silverado
   Trail between [60]Yountville and [61]Oakville, California.^[62][5]. The
   company also labels under the names "Decoy" and "Migration." Prices
   range from $30 to approximately $100.^[63][1]
