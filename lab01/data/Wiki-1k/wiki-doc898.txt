Ednyfed Hudson Davies

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

   Jump to: [6]navigation, [7]search

   (Gwilym) Ednyfed Hudson Davies (born [8]4 December [9]1929) is a
   [10]Welsh former politician.

   Hudson Davies was educated at [11]Dynevor [12]Grammar School,
   [13]Swansea, the [14]University College, Wales in Swansea, and
   [15]Balliol College, Oxford. He became a lecturer in government and a
   broadcaster.

   Davies was elected [16]Labour Party [17]Member of Parliament (MP) for
   [18]Conway in 1966, serving there until 1970. He was later elected for
   [19]Caerphilly, in 1979. In 1981, he was among the Labour MPs who
   defected to the new [20]Social Democratic Party.

   In 1983, in Caerphilly, it was the [21]Liberal side of the
   [22]SDP-Liberal Alliance which put up a candidate. Davies therefore did
   not stand in Caerphilly, but was adopted in [23]Basingstoke. He lost
   comprehensively as it was a strongly [24]Conservative seat. The
   Liberals also did not win in Caerphilly.

   Hudson-Davies remains active in public life as chairman of a museum
   trust in the New Forest.
