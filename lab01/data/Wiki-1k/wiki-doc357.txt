Drachenfels (Siebengebirge)

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

   Jump to: [6]navigation, [7]search
   Drachenfels in 1921.
   Ruin of castle Drachenfels.
   Old drawing of the Drachenfels.
   [8]Wine grown at the side of the Drachenfels.

   The Drachenfels ([9]German for Dragon's Rock; pronounced [10][danfls])
   is a mountain 321 metres (1,050 ft) in the [11]Siebengebirge mountain
   range near [12]Bonn, [13]Germany. The [14]castle ruin on top bears the
   same name. There is also another castle, which has recently been
   restored (2003), downstream the [15]Rhine called [16]Schloss
   Drachenburg.

   One German legend recounts, that [17]Siegfried the hero of the
   [18]Nibelungenlied killed a [19]dragon living in a cave in the
   mountain. After taking a bath in its blood, he became invulnerable.
   According to the legend, the dragon lived on that mountain, hence the
   mountain is named Drachenfels.

   A [20]mountain railway, the [21]Drachenfels Railway, connects
   [22]Knigswinter with the summit of the Drachenfels.
