Hidden file and hidden directory

   A hidden directory or hidden file on a computer is a [14]directory
   (folder) or [15]file which is not shown to the [16]user by default.
   Hidden directories are most often used to hide important [17]operating
   system-related files and user preferences. Unfortunately, this
   functionality can also be used by [18]malicious programs to hide their
   presence from unaware users.

[[23]edit] Unix and Unix-like

   In [24]Unix-like operating systems, hidden files' and directories'
   [25]names are prepended with [26]period (e.g. /home/user/.config). They
   are sometimes called dotfiles. How to show or hide them depends on the
   [27]file manager in use (e.g. [28]mc shows them by default) or with
   [29]ls the [30]flag -a can be used (ls -a).

[[31]edit] DOS

   In MS-DOS and other DOS systems, files and folders can be made hidden
   or visible by toggling their Hidden [32]File attribute using attrib
   command.

[[33]edit] Microsoft Windows

   In Microsoft Windows, hiding is similar to that of DOS - items can be
   made hidden or visible by toggling Hidden attribute, using attrib or
   [34]Windows Explorer.
