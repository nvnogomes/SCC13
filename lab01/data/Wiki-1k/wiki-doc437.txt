   Banksia undata, commonly known as Urchin Dryandra, is a [21]shrub
   endemic to [22]Western Australia.

   It was known as Dryandra praemorsa until 2007, when all Dryandra
   species were transferred to [23]Banksia by [24]Austin Mast and
   [25]Kevin Thiele. As there was already a plant named [26]Banksia
   praemorsa (Cut-leaf Banksia), Mast and Thiele were forced to chose a
   new specific epithet; their choice, "undata", is from the [27]Latin
   undatus ("undulate"), in reference to the wavy leaves.

   There are two varieties: [28]B. undata var. undata and [29]B. undata
   var. spendens.
