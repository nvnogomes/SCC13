Duderhof Heights

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

   Jump to: [6]navigation, [7]search

   Duderhof Heights or Duderhof Hills ([8]Russian: ) is a small highland
   area in the southwestern part of [9]Saint Petersburg ([10]Krasnoselsky
   District), to the south of the town of [11]Krasnoye Selo, on the
   northern edge of the [12]Izhora Plateau, which consists of several
   hills, most notably, the Orekhovaya hill ([13]Russian: , lit. Hazel
   Hill, [14]594151N 300755E / 59.6975N 30.13194E / 59.6975; 30.13194),
   the highest point of Saint Petersburg at 176 m (577'), in the south,
   and the Voronya hill ([15]Russian: , lit. Crow Hill, [16]594209N
   300738E / 59.7025N 30.12722E / 59.7025; 30.12722), 147 m (482'), in the
   north. Sometimes other smaller hills are considered part of the area as
   well: the Lysaya hill ([17]Russian: , lit. Bald Hill) further
   northward, the Kirchhof hill ([18]Russian: ) to the east, and the
   Kavelakhtinskaya ridge ([19]Russian: ) further to the south (however,
   all the smaller hills are situated in [20]Leningrad Oblast rather than
   in Saint Petersburg). Since 1992 the Orekhovaya and Voronya hills have
   been designated as protected natural area (of about 66 ha), divided by
   Sovetskaya Street of the settlement Mozhaysky into two parts. Nowadays
   both hills are mostly covered by [21]broadleaf forests (with [22]Acer
   platanoides, [23]Fraxinus excelsior, [24]Tilia cordata, [25]Ulmus
   glabra, [26]Quercus robur, [27]Corylus avellana), very uncommon for the
   region situated in the [28]taiga belt, and have a peculiar fauna and
   flora. The steep slopes of the hills and alkaline soils on the
   [29]limestone bedrock are also notable. For a critically endangered
   species of [30]weevil, Otiorhynchus rugosus Humm., this is the only
   known location in European Russia. This is also the only site in Saint
   Petersburg and Leningrad Oblast where [31]European Beech is able to
   overwinter in cultivation, albeit suffering considerable dieback.

   The vegetation on the hills hasn't remained intact and has been subject
   to heavy anthropogenic influence for the last two centuries. In the
   first half of the 19th century a [32]landscape garden was laid down on
   the Orekhovaya hill. During the [33]Siege of Leningrad (1941-1944) the
   battlefront ran through the hills and they became almost completely
   [34]deforestated. German artillery positions shelling the city were
   situated on the Voronya hill. The hills are still a popular [35]ski
   resort. The surrounding area is totally devoid of forests and mostly
   built up. The nearest railway station, Mozhayskaya (formerly Duderhof)
   of the Ligovo-Krasnoye Selo-[36]Gatchina railroad (constructed in
   1859), is situated immediately to the west of the hills.
