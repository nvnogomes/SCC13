   Dogora or Dagora, the Space Monster, released in Japan as Uch Daikaij
   Dogora (^[42]?, lit. "Giant Space Monster Dogora"), is a [43]tokusatsu
   [44]science fiction [45]film produced and released by [46]Toho Studios
   in [47]1964. It was directed by the "Golden Duo" of director [48]Ishir
   Honda and special-effects director [49]Eiji Tsuburaya.

[[57]edit] Plot

   Several satellites are destroyed and only a few days later, a group of
   diamond thieves find that the gems they stole suddenly disappear. The
   two incidents are linked as scientists discover giant jellyfish-like
   creatures, mutated from the high levels of radiation over Japan, are
   consuming all carbon based matter. The creatures start to attack
   civilian structures such as bridges and ships. The scientists must find
   a way to stop the creatures before it's too late.

[[91]edit] Trivia

     * Dogora is [92]Toho's first space monster, and it is also Toho's
       last original monster in which it appeared alone.

     * The lines "I am young soldier" by Dr. Munakata are parodies of the
       lines by the President of the then Japanese National Railways.

     * [93]Robert Dunham appears by the name of [94]Dan Yuma in this film.

     * [95]Waka-To -hashi bridge, the bridge that Dogora lifted, was
       completed two years before this film was made.

     * [96]Akira Ifukube uses a [97]musical saw in the score for this
       film.

     * Dogora appeared in the TV show, [98]Godzilla Island, as a spawned
       monster by the Xians.
