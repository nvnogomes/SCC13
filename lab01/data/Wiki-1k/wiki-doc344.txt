   Narana Sinai Coissor (b. [14]October 3, [15]1933) is a [16]Portuguese
   [17]lawyer and politician of the right-wing [18]Democratic and Social
   Center / People's Party ([19]Portuguese: Centro Democrtico e
   Social/Partido Popular - CDS/PP).

   Of [20]Hindu [21]Goan origin, by which he has [22]blue eyes, Narana
   Coissor is a former deputy to the [23]Assembly of the Republic (the
   Portuguese Parliament; see [24]Politics of Portugal) and is presently
   president of the [25]Institute of Oriental Studies, in [26]Lisbon.

   He is married to Teresa da Costa Brando, born at [27]Caldas da Rainha,
   by whom he has a daughter Smit da Costa Brando Coissor.
