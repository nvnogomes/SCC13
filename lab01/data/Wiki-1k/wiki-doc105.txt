Nahr al-Kalb

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

     (Redirected from [6]Dog River (Lebanon))
   Jump to: [7]navigation, [8]search

   The Nahr al-Kalb ([9]Arabic: ) (Rendered in [10]English Al-Kalb River,
   but translated Dog River) is a [11]river in [12]Lebanon. It runs for 19
   miles from a spring near the [13]Jeita Grotto to the [14]Mediterranean
   Sea.

[[19]edit] Historical significance

   A 14th century BC treaty, concluded by [20]Ramses II, set the Nahr
   al-Kalb as the border between [21]Egypt and the possessions of the
   [22]Hittites.

   Past generals and conquerors have traditionally built [23]monuments at
   the mouth of the Nahr al-Kalb. Ramses II, [24]Nebuchadnezzar,
   [25]Esarhaddon, [26]Marcus Aurelius, and even armies from modern-day
   [27]France and [28]Great Britain have engaged in this practice. In
   1946, a monument was erected in celebration of Lebanons
   [29]independence, which came in 1943. In 2000, Lebanese demonstrators,
   including supporters of ex-general and current politician [30]Michel
   Aoun, unsuccessfully attempted to erect two new monuments, one marking
   the departure of [31]Israeli troops from Lebanon, and the other
   anticipating the withdrawal of [32]Syrian forces.

   In 2005, a commemorative stela at the river was listed in the
   [33]UNESCO [34]Memory of the World initiative.
