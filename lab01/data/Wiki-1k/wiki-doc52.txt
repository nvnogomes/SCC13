   Dizzy Prince of the Yolkfolk is an [29]adventure [30]video game
   published in December 1991 by [31]Codemasters for the [32]Amstrad CPC,
   [33]Atari ST, [34]Commodore 64, [35]ZX Spectrum, [36]DOS, [37]NES and
   [38]Amiga. It was the sixth game in the [39]Dizzy adventure series.
   Initially it was only released as part of the Dizzy's Excellent
   Adventures compilation.

   The creators of the series, the [40]Oliver Twins outsourced the video
   game company [41]Big Red Software to design and develop this game.

   This was probably the smallest Dizzy adventure game in terms of the
   number of rooms, but this meant that it was maybe the game with most
   critical acclaim. Magazine [42]Amstrad Action was noted in its dislike
   of walking from one area of the map to another to complete problems
   which it said was tedious. The smaller map helped to counteract this.
   Reputed to have been commissioned, designed, developed and completed in
   less than a month.

   The game in November 1992 was enhanced and re-released with the
   illfated [43]Aladdin Deck Enhancer under the title, Dizzy The
   Adventurer. The title was then released in 1993 as part of the [44]The
   Excellent Dizzy Collection.

   The game interface and mechanics resemble those of [45]Magicland Dizzy,
   discarding changes introduced in [46]the fifth game.
