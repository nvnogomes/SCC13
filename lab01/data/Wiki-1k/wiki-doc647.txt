The European Law Students' Association

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

     (Redirected from [6]ELSA - The European Law Students' Association)

   The logo of The European Law Students' Association

   The European Law Students' Association (often called ELSA) is an
   international, independent, non-political, non-profit-making
   organisation run by and for law students. It is comprised of law
   students and recent graduates who are interested in law and have
   demonstrated commitment to international issues.

   ELSA is today the world's largest independent law students' association
   and it is represented in more than 200 law faculties in 36 countries
   across Europe with membership in excess of 30,000 law students and
   young lawyers.

[[16]edit] History

   ELSA was founded by law students from [17]Austria, [18]Hungary,
   [19]Poland and [20]West Germany on [21]4 May [22]1981.

   ELSA has through its activities and commitment within the international
   community gained a special status with several international
   institutions.

   ELSA has gained consultative status with several United Nations bodies;
   in 1997 ELSA obtained Special Consultative Status with UN [23]ECOSOC
   (United Nations Economic and Social Council) and Consultative Status
   with [24]UNCITRAL (UN Commission on International Trade Law), in 1994
   ELSA was granted Consultative Status in Category C in [25]UNESCO (UN
   Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organisation). Furthermore, in
   2000 ELSA was granted Consultative Status (recently called
   Participatory Status) with the [26]Council of Europe. In addition, ELSA
   has a co-operation agreement with [27]UNHCR (UN High Commissioner for
   Refugees). In October 2005 ELSA has obtained Observer Status with
   [28]WIPO (World Intellectual Property Organization).

[[29]edit] Objectives

   The idea behind ELSA is to enable all students to have access to the
   same information, lectures, seminars, social events, international
   opportunities and exposure to different legal cultures. The UK has a
   very strong tradition of student law societies based at individual
   universities and law schools. ELSA is not intended to replace these
   societies as it is an association and membership is not restricted to
   students from any one particular institution. ELSA strives for a
   co-operative and collaborative relationship with law societies and in
   this regard, has chosen to adopt an [30]ALSA model.

[[31]edit] ELSA network

   ELSA is a unique and growing network of 30,000 young people from more
   than 200 universities in 36 countries across Europe. ELSA currently has
   members and observers in: [32]Austria, [33]Belgium, [34]Bulgaria,
   [35]Croatia, [36]Czech Republic, [37]Denmark, [38]Estonia, [39]Finland,
   [40]France, [41]Georgia, [42]Germany, [43]Greece, [44]Hungary,
   [45]Iceland, [46]Ireland, [47]Italy, [48]Kazakhstan, [49]Latvia,
   [50]Lithuania, [51]Malta, [52]Montenegro, [53]the Netherlands,
   [54]Norway, [55]Poland, [56]Portugal, [57]Republic of Macedonia,
   [58]Romania, [59]Russian Federation, [60]Serbia, [61]Slovakia,
   [62]Slovenia, [63]Spain, [64]Sweden, [65]Switzerland, [66]Turkey, and
   [67]Ukraine.

   Furthermore, ELSA co-operates with other student organisations across
   the world, for instance ILSA in North America, ALSA in Japan, [68]ALSA
   in Australia, ALSA in South Africa and AEJCI in Ivory Coast.

[[69]edit] Activities

   ELSA's activities fall into three key areas:

   S&C - Seminars & Conferences
     * Lectures
     * Panel discussions
     * Seminars and conferences (both local and international)
     * Law schools
     * Study visits
     * Institutional visits

   AA - Academic Activities
     * "Lawyers at Work" (L@W) events
     * Study abroad programme
     * Legal research group
     * Essay competitions
     * Moot courts
     * [70]ELSA Moot Court Competition on WTO Law

   STEP - Student Trainee Exchange Programme
     * International traineeships programme for law students
