Ebenezer J. Shields

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

   Jump to: [6]navigation, [7]search

   Ebenezer J. Shields was an [8]American politician that represented
   [9]Tennessee's [10]tenth district in the [11]United States House of
   Representatives.

[[12]edit] Biography

   He was born in [13]Georgia on [14]December 22, [15]1778. He moved to
   Tennessee in 1809 and settled on Robertson Fork Creek near
   [16]Lynnville. He completed preparatory studies and graduated from the
   University of [17]Nashville, Tennessee in 1827. He studied law, was
   admitted to the [18]bar, and commenced practice in [19]Pulaski,
   Tennessee. Between 1833 and 1835, he was a member of the [20]Tennessee
   House of Representatives.

   He was elected as a [21]White supporter to the [22]Twenty-fourth
   Congress by the tenth district of Tennessee and re-elected as a
   [23]Whig. He was an unsuccessful candidate for re-election in 1938 to
   the [24]Twenty-sixth Congress. He served from [25]March 4, [26]1835 to
   [27]March 3, [28]1839. He resumed the practice of law in Pulaski and
   moved to [29]Memphis, Tennessee in 1844, where he continued the
   practice of his profession. He died on [30]April 21, [31]1846 near
   [32]La Grange, Texas.
