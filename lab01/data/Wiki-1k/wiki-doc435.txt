Dry punch

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

   Jump to: [6]navigation, [7]search

   A dry punch is [8]meteorological slang for a surge of drier [9]air,
   normally a [10]synoptic scale or [11]mesoscale process. A dry punch at
   the surface results in a [12]dry line bulge. A dry punch aloft above an
   area of moist air at low levels often increases the potential for
   [13]severe weather.
