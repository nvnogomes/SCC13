   Doppelgnger is the second full-length album from [27]Mukilteo,
   Washington's [28]The Fall of Troy, which followed up the band's
   bootlegged Ghostship demos from [29]2004 as well as their
   [30]self-titled LP from [31]2003. Four of the songs ("I Just Got This
   Symphony Goin'", "[32]F.C.P.R.E.M.I.X.", "Mouths Like Sidewinder
   Missiles", "Whacko Jacko Steals the Elephant Man's Bones") were
   re-recorded versions of tracks from the self-titled album, hence the
   name [33]Doppelgnger. "Macaulay McCulkin" was taken from the
   [34]Ghostship EP.

[[41]edit] References to popular culture

     * "You Got a Death Wish, Johnny Truant?" and "The Hol[ ]y Tape..."
       are quotes taken from [42]Mark Z. Danielewski's novel [43]House of
       Leaves. "Hol[ ]y" being short for "Holloway"
     * In live performances, Erak has been known to finish the song "The
       Hol[ ]y Tape..." with instrumentation from Disney movie
       [44]Aladdin's song, "[45]A Whole New World"[46].
     * The title "Laces Out, Dan!" is taken from a quote in the film
       [47]Ace Ventura: Pet Detective. In the movie, the person in
       reference is Miami Dolphins player Dan Marino.
     * The title of the song "Tom Waits" is in reference to a chapter in
       [48]House of Leaves by [49]Mark Z. Danielewski; the singer [50]Tom
       Waits also has a song named "The Fall of Troy".
     * "[51]F.C.P.R.E.M.I.X." is featured on the soundtrack of the video
       games [52]MLB 2K6, [53]Saint's Row, and [54]Guitar Hero III:
       Legends of Rock.
     * "Whacko Jacko Steals the Elephant Man's Bones" is a reference to
       the music video of [55]Michael Jackson's "[56]Leave Me Alone" in
       which Michael Jackson dances along side a claymation portrayal of
       [57]Joseph Merrick's, the Elephant Man's, bones.
