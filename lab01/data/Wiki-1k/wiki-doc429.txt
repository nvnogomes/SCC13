Blue diaper syndrome

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

     (Redirected from [6]Drummond's syndrome)
   Jump to: [7]navigation, [8]search

   Blue diaper syndrome is a rare, [9]autosomal recessive [10]metabolic
   disorder characterized in infants by bluish urine-stained diapers.

   It is caused by a defect in [11]tryptophan absorption. Bacterial
   degradation of tryptophan in the intestine leads to excessive
   [12]indole production and thus to [13]indicanuria which, on oxidation
   to [14]indigo blue, causes a peculiar bluish discoloration of the
   diaper. Symptoms typically include digestive disturbances, fever and
   visual problems.

[[15]edit] Genetics

   Blue diaper syndrome has an autosomal recessive pattern of inheritance.

   Blue diaper syndrome is thought to be inherited as an [16]autosomal
   recessive disorder. Recent research indicates that [17]mutations in the
   [18]LAT2 and [19]TAT1 [20]genes might be involved in causing this
   syndrome.
