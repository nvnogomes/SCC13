Dore Abbey

   Dore Abbey
   The [8]Green Man roof boss from Dore Abbey

   Dore Abbey, [9][1] in [10]Herefordshire, at [11]grid reference
   [12]SO387303, near the village of [13]Abbey Dore in the [14]Golden
   Valley is a former [15]Cistercian [16]Abbey.

[[17]edit] History

   It was founded in 1147 by Lord [18]Robert d'Ewyas and, in 1282,
   dedicated to the [19]Holy Trinity and [20]Saint Mary, by D'Ewyas'
   [21]brother in law, [22]Thomas Cantilupe, a [23]Bishop of Hereford
   [24][2].

   It is located in the [25]Golden Valley of the [26]River Dore. The
   [27]abbey was [28]dissolved in 1536. Part of the original abbey
   [29]church, begun in about 1180, is still in use. The surviving
   building, constructed of Herefordshire [30]Old Red Sandstone, lacks its
   original [31]nave. It was restored in the 1630s, having been left in
   ruins following the [32]Dissolution. Its rescuers were the Scudamores,
   a local [33]gentry family historically connected with [34]Owain Glyndr
   in the fifteenth century and made [35]Viscounts by the 1600s. They
   employed [36]John Abel to repair the woodwork, and the roof of the
   [37]chancel and decorated carved screen [38][3] are his work.
