Donato da Cascia

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

     (Redirected from [6]Donato da Florentia)
   Jump to: [7]navigation, [8]search

   Donato da Cascia (also da Firenze or da Florentia) (fl. c. 1350 1370)
   was an [9]Italian composer of the [10]Trecento. All of his surviving
   music is secular, and the largest single source is the [11]Squarcialupi
   Codex. He was probably also a priest, and the picture that survives of
   him in the Squarciulupi Codex shows him in the robes of the
   [12]Benedictine order.

   Nothing at all is known about his life except what can be inferred from
   his picture, his name, and the geographic distribution of his surviving
   music. He was probably from [13]Cascia, near [14]Florence, and all of
   his music, with one exception (the virelai), is found in sources in
   [15]Tuscany.

   Seventeen compositions by Donato survive, including: fourteen
   [16]madrigals, one [17]caccia, one [18]virelai, and one [19]ballata.
   Except for one piece, his music is all for two voices, typical of
   mid-century practice in that regard, but unusually virtuosic; according
   to [20]Nino Pirrotta, it "represents the peak of virtuoso singing in
   the Italian madrigal, and therefore in the Italian [21]Ars nova as a
   whole." ^[22][1]

   Donato's madrigals usually feature an upper voice part which is more
   elaborate than the lower, and often use [23]imitation between the two
   voices, though usually the imitative passages are short. In addition he
   uses repeated words and phrases, often with a humorous intent; the
   influence of the caccia is evident in this device. Jacopo da Bologna
   was probably an influence on his work, as can be seen in the
   single-voiced transitional passages between different verses of the
   madrigals, typical of Jacopo.
