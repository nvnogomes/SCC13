Frank Ellsworth Doremus

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

     (Redirected from [6]Doremus, Frank E.)
   Jump to: [7]navigation, [8]search

   Frank Ellsworth Doremus ([9]August 31, [10]1865 - [11]September 4,
   [12]1947) was a politician from the [13]U.S. state of [14]Michigan.

[[15]edit] Biography

   Doremus was born in [16]Venango County, Pennsylvania. He attended the
   public schools of [17]Portland, Michigan and graduated from [18]Detroit
   College of Law.

   He established the [19]Portland Review in 1885, editing it until 1899.
   He was member of the [20]Michigan House of Representatives from
   [21]Ionia County 1st District serving from 1890 to 1892 and
   [22]postmaster of Portland from 1895 to 1899. He was admitted to the
   bar and commenced practice in [23]Detroit in 1899. He was assistant
   corporation counsel of Detroit from 1903 to 1907. He was city
   [24]comptroller 1907-1910.

   In 1910, Doremus defeated incumbent [25]Republican [26]Edwin C. Denby
   to be elected as a [27]Democrat from [28]Michigan's 1st congressional
   district to the [29]Sixty-second and to the four succeeding
   [30]Congresses, serving from [31]March 4, [32]1911 to [33]March 3,
   [34]1921. He was a delegate to [35]Democratic National Convention from
   Michigan in 1916 and 1920. He served as mayor of Detroit in 1923 until
   he resigned the following year. He resumed the practice of law in
   [36]Fowlerville, Michigan.

   Frank Ellsworth Doremus died in [37]Howell, Michigan and was interred
   in [38]Roseland Park, Detroit, Michigan.
