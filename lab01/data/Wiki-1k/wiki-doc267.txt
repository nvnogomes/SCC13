Double Bluff Beach Access

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

   Jump to: [6]navigation, [7]search

   Double Bluff Beach Access and [8]Double Bluff State Park in the [9]U.S.
   state of [10]Washington is considered by many to be the best beach on
   the Southern end of Whidbey Island with its offering of shellfish
   harvest and at times total solitude.

[[16]edit] Geography

   Located in the unincorpated [17]Freeland area of [18]Island County,
   Washington along the shores of [19]Useless Bay, [20]Admiralty Inlet,
   and Double Bluff to the North of the Puget Sound. The beach access
   parking lot is roughly two miles from the retail core of [21]Freeland.
   The Olympic Mountains can be seen from the sections of shore along
   [22]Admiralty Inlet. To the SSE of the shore one can see the highrises
   of [23]Seattle roughly 30 miles away dwarfed by [24]Mt. Rainier which
   is roughly 100 miles away.

   Double Bluff seen from the South in the mid [25]littoral zone.

[[26]edit] Geology

   On the Southern end of the shore is [27]Useless Bay which faces South
   towards the [28]Puget Sound and [29]Seattle. Being a sandy bay fed by
   bluffs at its East and West ends, the bay has a shallow slope and the
   horizontal distance between high and lows tidelines can exceed 2500
   feet.

   Past the Western end of Useless Bay begins [30]Admiralty Inlet and the
   shore here abuts a large bluff which rises from [31]Useless Bay and
   descends to [32]Double Bluff several miles down the shore. The primary
   character of the shore here is shifting sands eroded from the bluffs
   with large fields of rocks, slabs of [33]peat, and erratic
   [34]boulders.

   Due to its island locality the shores here do not have railroad tracks
   present allowing one to witness over time the erosive processes that
   have been at work here since the last ice age.

   Different geologic strata can be observed in the exposed face of the
   bluff.

[[35]edit] Wildlife

   Many species of wildlife may be found here including [36]bald eagles,
   [37]great blue herons, [38]peregrine falcons, [39]gulls, [40]crows,
   several species of [41]sea anemone, many species of [42]crab,
   [43]snails, [44]sea urchins, and [45]barnacles.

[[46]edit] Activities

     * Walking
     * Tidepool Exploration
     * Birdwatching
     * Swimming
     * Skimboarding
     * Picnicking
     * Clamming (with license)
