   Duino-Aurisina (in [26]Slovenian Devin-Nabreina,in [27]German
   Tybein-Naberschin) is a [28]comune (municipality) in the [29]Province
   of Trieste in the [30]Italian region of [31]Friuli-Venezia Giulia.
   Lying near the border with [32]Slovenia, it has a substantial Slovene
   population.

[[41]edit] Geography

   The town lies on the Adriatic coast about 15 km northwest of
   [42]Trieste, on the border with Slovenia/Slovenija.

   Duino-Aurisina borders the following municipalities: [43]Komen
   (Slovenia/Slovenija), [44]Doberd del Lago, [45]Monfalcone, [46]Seana
   (Slovenia/Slovenija), [47]Sgonico, [48]Trieste.

[[49]edit] Famous residents

   Slovene writer [50]Alojz Rebula was born in San Pelagio. The poet
   Rainer Maria Rilke began his Duino Elegies while staying at Duino
   castle as a guest, in 1912.

[[51]edit] Parishes

   The municipality of Duino-Aurisina contains the [52]frazioni
   (subdivisions, mainly villages and hamlets). This list shows Italian
   names with Slovene names in brackets: [53]Aurisina (until the rise of
   [54]fascism Nabresina) (Nabreina) , Ceroglie (Cerovlje), [55]Duino
   (Devin), Malchina (Mavhinje), Medeazza (Medja vas) , Precenico (Prenik)
   , Prepotto (Praprot), [56]San Pelagio (empolaj), San Giovanni di Duino
   (tivan), [57]Sistiana (Sesljan), Slivia (Slivno), Ternova Piccola
   (Trnovca), Villaggio del Pescatore (Ribiko naselje), Visogliano
   (Viovlje), Aurisina S. Croce (Nabreina Kri), Aurisina Cave (Nabreina
   Kamnolomi), Aurisina Stazione (Nabreina Postaja) and Aurisina Centro
   (Nabreina).

[[58]edit] Demographic evolution

   As of [59]31 December [60]2004, it had a population of 8,815 and an
   area of 45.2 km. According to the 1971 census, 37,5% of the population
   (mainly in and around Aurisina) belongs to the [61]Slovenian ethnic
   group and about 60 % to the [62]Italian ethnic group (mainly in Duino
   and Sistiana) ^[63][1]
