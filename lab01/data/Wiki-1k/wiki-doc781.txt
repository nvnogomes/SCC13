   Easy Come, Easy Go is a studio [23]album by [24]English
   [25]singer-songwriter [26]Marianne Faithfull, which was released in the
   [27]EU on November 10, 2008. The album is produced by [28]Hal Willner
   and features guest appearances from a variety of musicians. It was
   released as both a standard 10-track CD and a special 18-track
   edition^[29][1] with a [30]DVD documentary by Anne Rohart under
   [31]Jean-Baptiste Mondino's artistic direction, with both Faithfull and
   Wilner commenting on the song selection. A collectible 2-disc [32]vinyl
   pressing is also available. The album was recorded into Protools in
   [33]NYC at the Sear Sound Studio.

[[39]edit] Release

   The EU release on Naive was November 10, 2008.^[40][2] [41]Amazon began
   US shipment of the vinyl pressing as an import November 25 and the
   10-song CD also as an import, on December 2. Amazon began US shipment
   of the CD-DVD version on December 9, 2008.

   On January 20, 2009 the blog on Faithfull's MySpace page announced:
   "The US, UK and Australian release dates for Marianne's new album are
   as follows...US release: March 17, 2009 on [42]Decca,^[43][3] UK
   release: March 16, 2009 on [44]Dramatico, Australian release: February
   14, 2009 on [45]Shock Records.^[46][2]

[[47]edit] Track listing

   Disc 1:
    1. "Down from Dover", (originally by [48]Dolly Parton)
    2. "Hold On, Hold On", with [49]Cat Power (originally by [50]Neko
       Case)
    3. "[51]Solitude" (originally by [52]Duke Ellington & [53]Eddie
       DeLange)
    4. "The Crane Wife 3", with [54]Nick Cave (originally by [55]The
       Decemberists)
    5. "Easy Come, Easy Go" (originally by [56]Bessie Smith)
    6. "Children of Stone", with [57]Rufus Wainwright (originally by
       [58]Espers)
    7. "How Many Worlds", with [59]Teddy Thompson (originally by [60]Brian
       Eno)
    8. "In Germany Before the War" (originally by [61]Randy Newman)
    9. "Ooh Baby Baby", with [62]Antony Hegarty (originally by [63]Smokey
       Robinson)
   10. "Sing Me Back Home", with [64]Keith Richards (originally by
       [65]Merle Haggard)

   Disc 2:
    1. "Salvation", with [66]Sean Lennon (originally by [67]Black Rebel
       Motorcycle Club)
    2. "[68]Black Coffee" (originally by [69]Sarah Vaughan)
    3. "The Phoenix", with [70]Kate and Anna McGarrigle (originally by
       [71]Judee Sill)
    4. "Dear God Please Help Me" (originally by [72]Morrissey)
    5. "Kimbie" (originally by [73]Jackson C. Frank)
    6. "Many a Mile to Freedom", with Jenni Muldaur (originally by
       [74]Traffic)
    7. "[75]Somewhere (A Place for Us)", with [76]Jarvis Cocker
       (originally by [77]Leonard Bernstein and [78]Stephen Sondheim)
    8. "Flandyke Shore", with Kate and Anna McGarrigle (traditional, but
       sung famously by [79]Nic Jones)
