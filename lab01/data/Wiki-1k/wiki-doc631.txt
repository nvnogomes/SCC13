EEsof

   Jump to: [6]navigation, [7]search

   information might be found on the [9]talk page. (January 2008)

   EEsof was founded in 1983 by an entrepreneur, [10]Charles J. ("Chuck")
   Abronson, and a former [11]Compact Software employee, [12]Bill
   Childs^[13][1] ^[14][2] ^[15][3]. Its [16]EDA products included high
   frequency circuit simulators such as Touchstone and Libra. Although the
   Touchstone simulator itself is obsolete^[17][4], its [18]eponymous file
   format lives on. EEsof was acquired by [19]Hewlett-Packard in 1993
   ^[20][5] and later spun out as part of [21]Agilent Technologies in 1999
   thus becoming Agilent EEsof EDA^[22][6].

   After the merger of HP and EEsof, the EEsof products were combined with
   the HP simulator, Microwave Design System (MDS). MDS was first marketed
   in 1985 but the project actually predated EEsof: It evolved from a
   project by Les Besser when he was with HP^[23][7]. The Touchstone UI
   was a textual netlist, whereas MDS had the first schematic capture UI
   in RF EDA.

   The HP and EEsof harmonic balance simulators also had parallel lives
   before the merger. HP funded an employee Ken Kundert to do a PhD at UC
   Berkeley PhD. For his thesis he developed Spectra, the first harmonic
   balance prototype. Since Berkeley has an open policy to all of its
   research work, EEsof was able to learn about the project and actually
   released a product before HP was able to commercialise it in MDS.
   (Libra was a play on the Latin word libra for balance or scales). Ken
   left HP to join Cadence shortly after receiving his PhD to develop
   Spectre and SpectreRF.

   Current products are [24]Advanced Design System (ADS) RF EDA software,
   [25]GoldenGate RFIC Simulator, [26]IC-CAP (pronounced "icecap" or
   "eye-cee-cap") device modeling software and systems,
   [27]Electromagnetic Professional (EMPro), [28]Momentum,
   [29]Electromagnetic Design System (EMDS), [30]Genesys, [31]SystemVue,
   [32]RF Design Environment (RFDE), and [33]Antenna Modeling Design
   System (AMDS). The SystemVue and Genesys products were added as a
   result of the acquisition of [34]Eagleware-[35]Elanix in 2005
   ^[36][8]^[37][9]. In turn Eagleware-Elanix was a result of the merger
   of Eagleware and Elanix^[38][10]. Eagleware itself was founded in 1985
   by [39]Randy Rhea ^[40][11].
