   The Black Bittern, Ixobrychus flavicollis, is a [25]bittern of Old
   World origin, breeding in tropical [26]Asia from [27]Pakistan,
   [28]India and [29]Sri Lanka east to [30]China, [31]Indonesia and
   [32]Australia. It is mainly resident, but some northern birds
   [33]migrate short distances.
   in flight in [34]Kolkata, [35]West Bengal, [36]India.

   This is a fairly large species at 58cm in length, with a longish neck
   and long yellow bill. The adult is uniformly black above, with yellow
   neck sides. It is whitish below, heavily streaked with brown. The
   juvenile is like the adult, but dark brown rather than black.

   Their breeding habitat is reedbeds. They nest on platforms of reeds in
   shrubs, or sometimes in trees. 3-5 eggs are laid. They can be difficult
   to see, given their [37]skulking lifestyle and reedbed habitat, but
   tend to fly fairly frequently, when the all black upperparts makes them
   unmistakable.

   Black Bitterns feed on [38]insects, [39]fish and [40]amphibians.

[[46]edit] Conservation Status

[[47]edit] Australia

   Black Bitterns are not listed as threatened on the Australian
   [48]Environment Protection and Biodiversity Conservation Act 1999.

[[49]edit] State of Victoria, Australia

     * The Black Bittern is listed as threatened on the Victorian
       [50]Flora and Fauna Guarantee Act (1988).^[51][1] Under this Act,
       an Action Statement for the recovery and future management of this
       species has not yet been prepared.^[52][2]

     * On the 2007 advisory list of threatened vertebrate fauna in
       Victoria, the Black Bittern is listed as [53]vulnerable.^[54][3]
