Edward R. Burke

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

   Jump to: [6]navigation, [7]search
   For other persons named Edward Burke, see [8]Edward Burke
   (disambiguation).

   Edward Raymond Burke (1880 1968) was a [9]Nebraska [10]Democratic Party
   politician. Born in Running Water in [11]Bon Homme County, South Dakota
   on November 28, 1880. Moved to [12]Sparta, Wisconsin with his parents
   and then Beloit, Wisconsin where he went to [13]Beloit College.
   Graduated in 1906, moved to [14]Chadron, Nebraska where he taught
   school until 1908. He graduated from [15]Harvard Law School and was
   admitted to the bar in 1911. Afterwards, he set up shop in [16]Omaha,
   Nebraska.

   During the [17]World War I he enlisted and served as a second
   lieutenant in the Air Service from 1917 to 1919. He served as the
   president of the board of education for Omaha from 1927 to 1930. He was
   elected to the Seventy-third Congress from Nebraska in 1933 and then
   ran for Senator from in 1934. Serving from January 3, 1935, to January
   3, 1941, he chaired the [18]U.S. Senate Committee on Claims. He failed
   to be renominated for the seat in 1940.

   He resumed his law practice in Omaha in 1941 and moved to
   [19]Washington, D.C. in 1942 to serve as president of the Southern Coal
   Producers Association until 1947. He was a Washington representative
   and general counsel for [20]Hawaiian Statehood Commission until 1950,
   when he retired to [21]Kensington, Maryland. He died on November 4,
   1968 and was interment in Fort Lincoln Mausoleum.
