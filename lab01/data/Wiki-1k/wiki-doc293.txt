   The rectouterine pouch (or rectouterine excavation, rectovaginal or
   Pouch of Douglas) is the extension of the [14]peritoneal cavity between
   the [15]rectum and back wall of the [16]uterus in the female [17]human
   body.

   The rectouterine pouch is used in the treatment of end-stage [18]renal
   failure in patients who are treated by [19]peritoneal dialysis. The tip
   of the dialysis catheter is placed into the deepest point of the pouch.

   In women it is the deepest point of the peritoneal cavity, posterior to
   (behind) the uterus and anterior to (in front of) rectum. (The pouch on
   the other side of the uterus is the [20]vesicouterine excavation.)

   It is near the posterior [21]fornix of the vagina.

   In men, the region corresponding to the rectouterine pouch is the
   [22]Rectovesical excavation, which lies between the [23]urinary bladder
   and rectum. (There is no equivalent to the vesicouterine excavation.)

[[31]edit] Pathology

   The rectouterine pouch, being the lowest part of the peritoneal cavity
   in a woman who is standing, is a common site for the spread of
   pathology such as [32]ascites, [33]tumour, [34]endometriosis, [35]pus,
   etc.

[[36]edit] Naming/Etymology

   It is also known by the names Douglas pouch, Douglas cavity, Douglas
   space and Douglas cul-de-sac.^[37][1]

   It is named after the [38]Scottish [39]anatomist Dr [40]James Douglas
   (16751742) who extensively explored this region of the female body.
   Three other nearby anatomical structures are also named for him - the
   [41]Douglas fold, the [42]Douglas line and the [43]Douglas septum.

[[44]edit] Sexual Response

   It is thought by some that when a woman becomes aroused, her [45]uterus
   will retract, making it possible for the rectouterine pouch to be
   stimulated through the end of the posterior wall of the [46]vagina,
   which could result in an intense [47]orgasm. In a sexual context, the
   rectouterine pouch is referred to as the Epicenter or Deep spot (in
   common with the [48]AFE zone), the PFE zone (Posterior fornix erogenous
   zone) or, most commonly, the Cul-de-sac.
