   Don't Come Knocking is a [20]2005 [21]film, a comedy-drama [22]road
   movie directed by German director [23]Wim Wenders and written by
   Wenders and actor/playwright [24]Sam Shepard. The two had previously
   collaborated on the film [25]Paris, Texas.

[[31]edit] Plot

   Shepard stars as Howard Spence, an aging, hard-living [32]Western movie
   star, who, disgusted with his life, flees by horse from the set of his
   latest western filming in the desert outside [33]Moab, [34]Utah. He
   hits the road looking for refuge in his past, traveling to his hometown
   of [35]Elko, [36]Nevada and, eventually, to [37]Butte, [38]Montana,
   looking for a woman ([39]Jessica Lange) he left behind twenty years
   before when he was filming a movie there. Spence is doggedly pursued by
   Mr. Sutter ([40]Tim Roth), a humorless representative of the insurance
   company insuring Spence's latest film: Mr. Sutter's job is to return
   Spence to the set to finish filming the movie. Also converging on Butte
   is a young woman named Sky ([41]Sarah Polley), returning her late
   mother's ashes to her hometown and conducting a search of her own.

[[42]edit] Actors and cameos

   The film features cameo appearances by [43]George Kennedy as a
   beleaguered movie director, and [44]Tim Matheson and [45]Julia Sweeney
   as movie producers. Also appearing briefly is Tom Farrell (from Wenders
   1980 [46]Lightning Over Water and the Screaming Man from Paris, Texas)
   as a high-school acquaintance who recognizes Howard along the way. The
   film also marks the first collaboration in 18 years between Shepherd
   and long-time partner Jessica Lange (since 1988's [47]Far North), as
   the two had an agreement never to be working at the same time, in order
   not to neglect their children.

[[48]edit] Photography

   The film was shot on [49]Super 35mm by [50]Franz Lustig. He and Wenders
   emphasized the influence of painter [51]Edward Hopper on the
   [52]cinematography.
