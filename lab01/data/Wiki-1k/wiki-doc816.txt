It's the economy, stupid

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

     (Redirected from [6]Economy stupid)
   Jump to: [7]navigation, [8]search

   "It's the economy, stupid" was a phrase in [9]American politics widely
   used during [10]Bill Clinton's successful [11]1992 presidential
   campaign against [12]George H.W. Bush. For a time, Bush was considered
   unbeatable because of foreign policy developments such as the end of
   the [13]Cold War and the [14]Persian Gulf War. The phrase, coined by
   Clinton campaign strategist [15]James Carville, refers to the notion
   that Clinton was a better choice because Bush had not adequately
   addressed the economy, which had recently undergone a [16]recession.

   In order to keep the campaign on message, Carville hung a sign in Bill
   Clinton's [17]Little Rock campaign headquarters that said:
    1. Change vs. more of the same
    2. The economy, stupid
    3. Don't forget health care.^[18][1]

   Although the sign was intended for an internal audience of campaign
   workers, the phrase became something of a slogan for the Clinton
   [19]election campaign. Clinton's campaign used the [20]recession to
   successfully unseat George H.W. Bush. In March 1991, days after the
   [21]ground invasion of Iraq, 90% of polled Americans approved of
   President Bush's job performance.^[22][2] Later the next year,
   Americans' opinions had turned sharply; 64% of polled Americans
   disapproved of Bush's job performance in August 1992.^[23][2]

   The phrase is repeated often in American [24]political culture, usually
   starting with the word "it's" and with commentators sometimes using a
   different word in place of "economy." Examples include "It's the
   deficit, stupid!"^[25][3] "It's the corporation, stupid!"^[26][4] "It's
   the math, stupid!"^[27][5] and "It's the voters, stupid!"^[28][6]
