Dye tracing

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

     (Redirected from [6]Dye tracer)
   Jump to: [7]navigation, [8]search

   Dye tracing is tracking and tracing various [9]flows using [10]dye
   added to the [11]liquid in question. The purpose of tracking may be an
   analysis of the flow itself, of the transport of something by the flow
   of the objects that convey the flow. It is an evolution of the
   ages-known float tracing method, which basically consists of throwing a
   [12]buoyant object into a waterflow to see where it goes or where it
   emerges.

   Dye tracking may be either qualitative, i.e., the presence of
   particular flow and its estimate, or quantitative, when the amount of
   the traced dye is measured by special instruments.

[[20]edit] Fluorescent dyes

   Fluorescein in the [21]Chicago River on the [22]St. Patrick's Day
   (added for celebration, rather than tracing)

   Often [23]fluorescent dyes are used for this purpose, especially in the
   following circumstances
     * Insufficient lighting (e.g., sewers or cave waters)
     * Precise quantitative data are required, measured by a
       [24]fluorometer
     * Very small amounts of the dye is allowed to be added ([25]1 part
       per trillion may be detected).

   [26]Fluorescein is among the first fluorescent dyes, developed in 1871.
   Its disodium salt under the trademark "[27]Uranine" was developed
   several years later and still remains among the best tracer
   dyes.^[28][1]

   Another popular tracer dye is [29]rhodamine.

[[30]edit] Quantitative tracing

   The first method for technology-assisted dye tracing was based on the
   [31]absorption of dye in [32]charcoal. Charcoal packets may be placed
   along the expected route of the flow, later the collected dye may be
   chemically extracted and its amount subjectively evaluated.

   [33]Filter fluorometers were the first devices that could detect dye
   concentrations beyond [34]human eye sensitivity.

   [35]Spectrofluorometers developed in the mid-1980s made it possible to
   perform advanced analysis of fluorescence.

[[36]edit] Applications

[[37]edit] Water tracing

   Typical application of [38]water flow tracing include: ^[39][2]
     * [40]Plumbing/[41]piping tracing
     * Leak detection
     * Checking for illegal tapping
     * [42]Pollution studies
     * Natural waterflow analysis ([43]rivers, [44]lakes, [45]ocean
       currents, [46]cave waterflows, [47]karst studies, [48]groundwater
       filtration, etc.)
     * [49]Sewer and [50]stormwater drainage analysis

[[51]edit] Medicine and biology

   Dye tracing may be used for the analysis of [52]blood circulation
   within various parts of the human or animal body. For example,
   [53]fluorescent angiography, a technique of analysis of circulation in
   [54]retina is used for diagnosing various eye diseases.

   With modern fluorometers, capable of tracking single fluorescent
   molecules, it is possible to track migrations of single cells
   [55]tagged by a fluorescent molecule (see [56]fluorescein in biological
   research). For example, the [57]fluorescent-activated cell sorting in
   [58]flow cytometry makes it possible to sort out the cells with
   attached fluorescent molecules from a flow.
