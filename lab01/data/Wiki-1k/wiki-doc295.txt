Douwe Aukes class minelayer

   General characteristics
   Displacement: 687  tonnes
   Length: 55  m
   Propulsion: 1175 hp engine
   Speed: 13  [11]knots
   Complement: 60

   The Douwe Aukes class was a class of two [12]minelayers of the
   [13]Royal Netherlands Navy, built to serve in the Dutch territorial
   waters and named after the naval hero [14]Douwe Aukes. The construction
   of the ships began in 1919 and was completed in 1922. The ships where
   build by the shipyard Gusto from [15]Schiedam. Both ships where still
   in service during the [16]Second World War and they both escaped to the
   [17]United Kingdom, the Douwe Aukes on [18]14 May and the Van Meerlant
   on [19]18 May [20]1940. The Van Meerlant did sunk in 1942 in British
   service after a mine explosion.
